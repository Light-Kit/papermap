# the international jobs overview — 254 roles across four regions

filter the vault to `International` and the F-1/OPT logic disappears entirely — the flag becomes **host-country work authorization for a PRC national**, and the answer is genuinely different in Shanghai, in Basel, and in Sydney. this is the numbers tour of those **254 full-time roles** — **147 industry FTE + 107 postdocs** — and how they split across four regions that do not behave alike.

## sponsorship, region by region

across the whole international half: **147 `✓` · 105 `?` · 2 `✗`**. but that average hides everything — the `✓` is concentrated, and where it sits tells you where to aim.

- **China — 56 roles, essentially all `✓`.** the one region where work authorization is simply not a question for you, and the comp-bio depth is real: BeiGene/BeOne computational science, ByteDance and Tencent AI-for-science, WuXi, BGI/MGI, Zai Lab, Insilico, plus the clinical-genomics shops. if you are open to going back, this is the no-friction option — the visa problem vanishes and the science is genuine.
- **UK & Europe — 106 roles.** the most prestigious science and the hardest fine print for a non-EU national. the bullseyes are the international-by-design hosts that relocate foreign hires — **EMBL/EMBL-EBI, DKFZ, the Crick, Roche/Novartis Basel** — while **AstraZeneca-UK and GSK explicitly state no sponsorship** (the two `✗` in the whole region). most of the rest sits at `?`: excellent science, the gate is whether the institute will carry the permit. ask first.
- **Singapore / Japan / Korea — 44 roles.** A*STAR and RIKEN are international-friendly and on-profile for genomics; a clean, navigable set if you want Asia outside China.
- **Canada / Australia — 48 roles.** Canada's research-worker permits are comparatively navigable (AbCellera, the Toronto AI-bio cluster, OICR, BC Cancer); Australia's flagship institutes (Garvan, WEHI, Peter Mac) do superb cancer work but read `?` on visa support for an external hire.

## industry vs postdoc, abroad

the role-type split is **147 industry FTE to 107 postdocs** — more balanced than the US half. the same trade applies but the immigration math changes: outside the US there is no H-1B lottery, so the FTE-vs-postdoc choice is driven more by the science and the institution's willingness to relocate you than by cap-exemption. in China the distinction barely matters for authorization; in Europe the international-by-design *institutes* (postdoc-heavy) are often the surer permit path than a national pharma FTE.

## status, and the same honesty caveat

**109 recurring / rolling, 66 anticipated — monitor, 57 open, 22 closed (recurs).** as on the US side, there are no live 2027–2029 reqs yet and international reqs rot just as fast, so closed 2026 postings were redowngraded with links pointed at the lab or careers page where the next one will appear. no dead link is shown as live; no URL is invented.
