# the US jobs overview — 581 roles, read for sponsorship

the full-time map argues the *strategy* across the whole world; this post zooms into the **United States** half and reads it by the numbers. filter the vault to `United States` and this is what you are looking at: **581 full-time roles** — **364 industry FTE** and **217 academic postdocs** — across six sectors, every one carrying a Visa-sponsor flag because for a PRC national that flag, not the science, is what reorders the list.

## the split that matters most: sponsorship

the headline numbers are **321 `✓` · 251 `?` · 9 `✗`**. that is not optimism — a `✓` means a real, sourced basis to sponsor a Chinese national, and it falls into two honest piles:

- **the cap-exempt postdocs.** US universities and nonprofit research institutes are H-1B **cap-exempt** — they sponsor *outside* the lottery, year-round, plus J-1. that turns the academic postdoc into the single most reliable full-time foothold in the US for a foreign national, not a consolation prize. there are 217 US postdocs, of which 199 are flagged `✓` on this basis — MSK, Dana-Farber, the Broad, Fred Hutch, Penn, the cancer centers — and many are dead-on for single-cell / spatial / HRD work.
- **the routine industry sponsors.** big, established pharma and big tech file H-1B as a matter of course: Genentech gRED, Novartis Oncology Data Science, AbbVie, BMS, Merck, Pfizer, Amgen, plus the NVIDIA / Illumina / Tempus tier. target the *first* PhD rung — "Scientist," "Bioinformatics Scientist," "Computational Biologist I" — even when the post says "Senior," because PhD+2 often clears their bar.

the **251 `?`** are mostly techbio startups that simply don't state a policy: ask about sponsorship in the first screen, because a small company that won't file an H-1B is a dead end no matter how good the science. and the **9 `✗`** are the genuinely bolted doors — DOE national labs and federal roles (citizenship/clearance), and **T32-funded training postdocs** (by statute US citizens / permanent residents only). they're flagged so you don't spend the application.

## how the 581 split by sector

- **academic / institute — 205 roles.** the cap-exempt engine; where most of the `✓` postdocs live.
- **ml-first biotech (techbio) — 181 roles.** the most companies, the most `?`; superb fits, sponsorship unstated.
- **pharma / big biotech — 74 roles.** fewer reqs but the densest `✓` and the cleanest first-rung Scientist titles.
- **diagnostics / sequencing / tools — 89 roles.** clinical-genomics and pipeline work; your NGS skills map cleanly.
- **big tech — bio/health — 22 roles.** NVIDIA BioNeMo, Microsoft, Google — strong names, real fit on the FM angle.
- **national lab — 10 roles.** flagged and largely closed (citizenship/clearance) — the `✗` cluster.

## what the status labels are telling you

**194 are open (a live req at harvest), 262 recurring / rolling, 76 anticipated — monitor, 27 closed (recurs)** (plus 22 status-unclear). the honest caveat carries over from the map: there are no live 2027–2029 reqs yet, and full-time reqs rot fast, so where a 2026 posting had already closed it was redowngraded to "closed (recurs)" with the link pointed at the team's careers page. no dead link is shown as live; no URL is invented.
