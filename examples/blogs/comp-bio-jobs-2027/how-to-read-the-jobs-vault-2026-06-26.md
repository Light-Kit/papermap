# how to read the jobs vault

This is the full-time tracker — **947 employers and 1,187 roles** spanning industry jobs (FTE) and academic postdocs, in the US and internationally. It is built for a computational-biology PhD with a Chinese (PRC) passport, currently in a US program, targeting a full-time start in the **2027–2029** window. The US-internships vault is a separate, near-term track; here is how this one works.

## the filter is US vs International

In the internships vault you filter by sector or region. Here the **modality filter is `United States` vs `International`** — because for a full-time job the first and largest fork is which immigration regime you are under. Everything finer-grained — the actual region (US, UK & Europe, China, Singapore/Japan/Korea, Canada/Australia), the sector, and the role type — lives in each card's **meta line**, not as the top filter. The **topic** filter works as before (`single-cell`, `spatial`, `comp-onc`, `genomics`, `ml-ai`, …) and additionally carries **`postdoc`** and **`industry-role`** so you can isolate the two role types.

## the Visa-sponsor flag (this replaces the CPT/OPT flag)

For full-time work, F-1 / CPT / OPT logic no longer decides anything — those govern student and post-graduation training, not a permanent job. Every role here instead carries a **Visa-sponsor flag for a PRC national**:

- **Visa-sponsor `✓`** — sponsorship is effectively available: a **China** role (no barrier for a PRC national); a US **university/nonprofit postdoc** (H-1B **cap-exempt** or J-1 — universities sponsor these year-round, outside the lottery); or a large employer that is a known, routine H-1B sponsor (most big pharma and big tech). Earned from the source, not assumed.
- **Visa-sponsor `?`** — sponsorship is unstated. Most industry startups and many non-US institutes sit here: the science may be perfect but you must confirm they will file/relocate before you invest. Ask in the first conversation.
- **Visa-sponsor `✗`** — no sponsorship: the posting says so (e.g. AstraZeneca-UK, GSK), or US **citizenship/clearance** is required (DOE national labs, federal roles), or the postdoc is on a **T32 training grant** (US citizens / permanent residents only by statute). Do not spend an application here.

The flag is deliberately conservative: a `✓` means a real, sourced basis for sponsorship, and the US cap-exempt-postdoc `✓` is the one most people overlook.

## industry FTE vs postdoc

Two role types share this vault because they are the two real exits from a PhD, and they trade off differently on immigration. **Industry FTE** (Computational Biologist, Bioinformatics/Research Scientist, ML Scientist, Data Scientist) pays more but, in the US, runs through the **H-1B lottery**. **Postdoc** pays academic wages but is **cap-exempt**, so the work-authorization question is largely solved and it feeds the O-1/EB-1 track. The meta line and the `postdoc` / `industry-role` topics let you filter to whichever you are weighing.

## status labels

- **open (live req)** — a specific full-time req was live when harvested.
- **recurring / rolling** — the employer hires this role continuously, or it is a standing program; the link is the team's careers page.
- **closed (recurs)** — a specific 2026 req that has since closed; the link was redirected to the team's careers page, and the role recurs.
- **anticipated — monitor** — no live req found; the link is the careers/lab page to watch.

## the honesty caveat

There are **no live 2027–2029 reqs here** — that hiring has not happened yet, and full-time reqs rot faster than internships (a posting can close in weeks). So every link points either to a currently-open role (to show the shape, level, and sponsorship reality of the real job) or to the employer's careers / lab page (where the next one will appear). Where a 2026 req had already expired at harvest, it was redowngraded to "closed (recurs)" with the link pointed at the careers page — **no dead link is shown as live, and no URL is invented**.

This is a preparation asset: it tells you which employers will actually sponsor you, where the cap-exempt floor is, and which doors are bolted shut — so that when you are a year out from defending, you already know where to aim.
