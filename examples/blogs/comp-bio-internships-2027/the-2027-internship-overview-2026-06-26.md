# the 2027 internship overview — the vault at a glance

the companion landscape essay argues *what to do*; this one just walks you through *what is in here*. it is the map read aloud: **282 organizations and 589 internship roles**, harvested off the 2026 cycle to target summer 2027, each tagged with a sector, the research topics it touches, and an F-1 eligibility flag. if you only read one post before opening the Resources tab, read this — then filter.

## the one caveat, up front

as of mid-2026 the **summer-2027 reqs mostly do not exist yet** — that cycle opens in waves from roughly september 2026 through march 2027. so nothing here is a live 2027 link. each row is a 2026 posting (or a recurring program's page) that tells you the *shape* the 2027 version will take: who hires, what the role looks like, when it opens, and whether an F-1 student can realistically get it. treat it as a target map and re-pull live links when the season opens.

## how the 589 roles split

six sectors, and they are not the same size or the same value to you:

- **pharma / big biotech — 198 roles.** the deepest and most F-1-navigable pool. genentech alone runs a family of computational-biology-and-medicine intern tracks; roche, abbvie, bms, merck, astrazeneca, j&j, takeda, sanofi all post real PhD comp-bio/bioinformatics reqs. this is the spine.
- **ml-first biotech / techbio — 147 roles.** the most companies, the fewest seats per company. insitro, recursion, tempus, generate, noetik, immunai and the rest each run one or two slots — superb intellectual fits doing the single-cell / spatial / perturbation work you do, but lottery tickets you usually win through a warm intro or a paper.
- **academic / institute — 129 roles.** broad, msk, dana-farber, fred hutch, st. jude, allen, flatiron, plus a long tail of cancer-center and health-system summer programs. scientifically dead-on — but watch the funding line: institution-funded PhD lab internships are usually fine, federally-funded SIPs usually are not.
- **big tech — bio/health — 61 roles.** nvidia's bio-foundation-model intern is a genuine, late-closing, on-profile US role; microsoft health futures and google research run health/bio research internships. a few headline "bio" reqs are mislabeled or mislocated — the vault marks those so you don't waste an application.
- **diagnostics / sequencing / tools — 36 roles.** natera, exact sciences, thermo, pacbio, twist, plus the spatial/single-cell tool makers. real bioinformatics pipelines, your NGS/single-cell skills map cleanly; more pipeline than discovery — a breadth play.
- **national lab — 18 roles.** oak ridge, berkeley/JGI, argonne, PNNL, los alamos and the NIH federal SIP. flagged, but mostly **closed to you** — these typically require US citizenship or a green card.

## the eligibility picture is the part most lists hide

every role carries an F-1 flag, set conservatively: **11 are `✓`** (source-confirmed open to F-1 / CPT-OPT), **422 are `?`** (policy unstated — verify before you invest), and **156 are `✗`** (citizenship/PR required, or the posting explicitly excludes sponsorship-dependent students — novartis's analytics PhD program is the textbook example). that 156 is the number a normal job board would let you discover only after you'd written the essay. here it's a badge: the national labs, the federal SIPs, and the handful of pharma programs that screen out CPT/OPT are marked so you can skip them on sight.

## where the bullseyes are

ten roles are pre-starred — the ones where an HRD / single-cell / spatial cancer-omics profile converts most reliably: **genentech's** three computational-biology-and-medicine tracks (incl. the immunology one), **abbvie's** AI/ML and immunology bioinformatics interns, **insitro's** ML and omics data-science interns, **nvidia's** bio-foundation-model intern, **biomarin's** computational-biology intern, and **caribou's** bioinformatics intern. start there, then widen by sector and by the topics you actually do.
