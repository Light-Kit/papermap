# big tech, bio/health — the 61 roles, zoomed in

this is the corner of the vault you asked to see up close: **61 internship roles across 11 organizations** — Amazon, Apple, ByteDance, the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative / CZ Biohub, Google DeepMind, Google Research, IBM Research, Meta FAIR, Microsoft Research, NVIDIA, and OpenAI. it is the most ML-heavy, foundation-model-centric sector in the whole tracker, and it rewards a very specific framing of who you are. read this before you apply, because the application strategy here is genuinely different from pharma.

## the one mental shift

in pharma you apply as a **computational biologist** — the wet-lab fluency and the cancer-omics domain knowledge are the point. in big tech you apply as a **machine-learning scientist who happens to have a real biology problem**. the reqs say "Applied Scientist," "Research Scientist Intern," "Student Researcher," "Member of Technical Staff" — and the bar is ML depth (representation learning, foundation models, generative modeling, evaluation) *plus* a domain you can speak to fluently. your HRD / single-cell / spatial work is the differentiator, but you have to package it as modeling, not as biology. lead with a method or a paper, not a CV.

## the three flavors of org (they are not the same application)

**1. frontier AI labs where "bio" is one application of a general research internship.** Google DeepMind (3), Google Research (5), Meta FAIR (8), OpenAI (2), Apple AIML/Health (3), Amazon AGI/AWS Science (6), and NVIDIA's general deep-learning umbrella. here you usually apply to a **broad PhD research internship** and then *match* into a team once you're in the pool — there often isn't a "computational biology" req to click. the FAIR Chemistry, Google "AI for Health / foundation models for human health," and Apple "ML Research Scientist, Health" postings are the closest to named bio teams; the rest you reach by signalling fit and getting routed there.

**2. dedicated bio/health research groups with explicit comp-bio reqs.** this is where you can actually click "apply" on a biology role:
- **Microsoft Research (8)** — the densest on-profile cluster in the sector. Health Futures runs named reqs for *ML & Computational Biology for the Immune System (Immunomics)*, *Biomedical AI for Rare Disease Genomics*, *Biomedical Platforms and Genomics*, *Precision Health (EHR + genomics)*; AI4Science runs molecular/biomolecular large-model and RL-for-drug-discovery interns; and there's a clean *Research Intern — Computational Biology*.
- **NVIDIA (7)** — the **Bio Foundation Model Research** intern (Santa Clara, the vault's one starred big-tech bullseye) and **BioNeMo R&D** (structure/drug-discovery FMs), plus a medical-imaging (MONAI/Clara) applied-research intern. genomics/protein/human-genetics FMs are the core.
- **IBM Research (8)** — *AI Healthcare & Life Sciences Intern* and the PhD Fellowship are the bio entries; AI-for-Math-and-Science and Quantum-chemistry are adjacent.
- **CZI / CZ Biohub (5)** — *Computational Biologist Intern (Biohub SF)*, *Research Scientist Intern — Single Cell (AI/ML)*, the *CZI Science Data Scientist* track, and Biohub NY's AI Research intern. nonprofit, single-cell-native, dead-on for your profile.
- **ByteDance Seed / AML AI-for-Science (6)** — *Research Scientist Intern, Computational Biology*, *Biomolecular Structure FMs*, *Scaling AI for Biology*, *Generative AI for Science*. notably, four of these are flagged **F-1 `✓`** — the most internationally-open set in the sector.

**3. the bullseyes.** if you only chase a handful, chase these: **NVIDIA Bio-FM** (★), **Microsoft Health Futures Immunomics + the genomics/rare-disease reqs**, **NVIDIA BioNeMo**, **ByteDance Seed Computational Biology** (`✓`), **CZ Biohub single-cell**, **Google "AI for Health" foundation models**, and **Amazon AWS Healthcare-AI**. these are the ones where a cancer-multi-omics PhD converts without having to disguise the biology.

## the F-1 picture is the part to plan around

across the 61: **4 are `✓`, 55 are `?`, and 2 are `✗`**. don't over-read the wall of `?` — big-tech internships are, in practice, among the *most* F-1-friendly anywhere: these are large employers that routinely take international PhD interns on CPT, and the `?` mostly means the specific req page just doesn't state a policy. the two `✗` are real and worth knowing: **IBM's *Accelerated Drug Discovery Intern* and *Research Summer Intern, Computational Biology (Industrial & Applied Genomics)*** read citizenship/PR-restricted. and the four **ByteDance Seed** `✓` roles are the ones that explicitly welcome international students. still verify CPT eligibility in the first recruiter screen — but you should treat this sector as broadly open, not closed.

## how competitive, and what they actually look for

this is the top of the quant/ML applicant pool — these roles pull from the same candidates as the pure-ML internships, so the science bar is high. what tips it your way is **a publishable ML angle on a real biological dataset**: a single-cell or spatial foundation-model project, a representation-learning result on multi-omics, an HRD-classifier framed as a modeling contribution. they want to see you can ship research, not just run pipelines. a paper the team already knows, or a warm intro to the specific lab (Health Futures, BioNeMo, Seed AI-for-Science, a Biohub group), is worth more than ten cold applications.

## timing and how to apply

these open **early and in waves** — frontier-lab PhD research internships and the NVIDIA/Microsoft bio reqs surface from roughly **September through winter** for a summer 2026 start, and several (Apple AIML, Amazon UTA, the general "PhD research internship" pools) run **rolling**. for your 2027 target, watch the same windows reopen in fall 2026. apply to the **named bio reqs directly** (Microsoft Health Futures, NVIDIA Bio-FM/BioNeMo, CZ Biohub, ByteDance Seed, IBM AI-HLS), and for the frontier labs apply to the **general PhD research internship** and name the bio team you want in your materials so the matching routes you correctly.

filter Resources by the **big-tech** sector and by the topics you actually do (`foundation-models`, `single-cell`, `genomics`, `ml-ai`), and start with the starred NVIDIA Bio-FM role. the **landscape** essay has the cross-sector strategy; the **F-1 CPT/OPT playbook** covers the work-authorization mechanics you'll want settled before you accept anything here.
