# how to read this vault

this is a target map for a US computational-biology PhD internship search, built for an F-1 candidate with a cancer multi-omics (HRD, single-cell, spatial) background. 620 organizations, 1,008 roles. here is how to use it. (this vault is **US internships only**. full-time jobs and academic postdocs — US *and* international — live in a separate **jobs vault**, where the filter is US vs International and eligibility is flagged as visa sponsorship, not F-1.)

## the two kinds of entries

- **org** cards are companies, institutes, and labs — one per organization, with its careers page and a one-line read on whether it hires comp-bio PhD interns.
- **position** cards are specific internship roles or recurring named programs. this is where the detail lives: focus, eligibility, status, and fit.

## filters

the **resources** view facets two ways:

- **sector** (the modality filter): `pharma` · `ml-biotech` · `big-tech` · `academic` · `national-lab` · `dx-tools` (diagnostics, sequencing & lab-tools). start here — the sectors behave very differently, as the landscape blog explains.
- **topic**: the research area — `single-cell`, `spatial`, `comp-onc`, `genomics`, `foundation-models`, `ml-ai`, and so on. filter to what you actually do.

the **topics** view groups every role by research area if you would rather browse by what the work is than by who the employer is.

## the F-1 flag (read this carefully)

every position shows one of three flags in its meta line:

- **F-1 ✓** — a posting or company FAQ explicitly accepts international / CPT / OPT students. earned, not assumed. there are only a handful.
- **F-1 ?** — the posting is silent on visa status. this is most roles. silence is not a no; it means verify per posting before you invest.
- **F-1 ✗** — citizenship or permanent residency is required, or the program explicitly excludes sponsorship-dependent students (DOE national labs, the NIH federal summer program, novartis analytics). do not spend applications here.

the flagging is deliberately conservative. when in doubt it downgrades, so a ✓ is meaningful and a ✗ is a real wall. the F-1 playbook blog explains the mechanics behind these.

## status labels

- **open (2026 cycle)** — was live in the 2026 round when harvested.
- **closed (2026 — recurs)** — the 2026 posting has closed; the program recurs, and the link points to the careers page so you can catch the 2027 reopening.
- **recurring program** — a standing named program rather than a single dated req.
- **anticipated 2027** — expected to run for 2027 based on history.

## the honesty caveat

there are **no live summer-2027 application links here, because they do not exist yet**. the 2027 cycle opens in waves from september 2026 through march 2027. every link in this vault points either to a 2026-cycle posting (to show you the shape and requirements of the real role) or to the organization's careers page (where the 2027 version will appear). where a specific 2026 posting had already expired at build time, the link was redirected to the careers page and the role marked closed — so nothing here is a dead link dressed up as live.

this is a preparation asset. its job is to tell you exactly where to look, who to rule out, and when to act — so that when the season opens you are applying in the first week instead of discovering the program in march.
