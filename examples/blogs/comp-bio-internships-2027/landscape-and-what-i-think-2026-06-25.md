# the landscape, and what i actually think you should do

this vault tracks 282 companies and labs and 593 internship roles, built by reading the 2026 cycle so we can target summer 2027. the honest headline first: as of mid-2026, **summer-2027 postings mostly do not exist yet**. the 2027 cycle opens in waves from roughly september 2026 through march 2027. so what is collected here is not a list of live 2027 links — it is the recurring shape the 2027 cycle will take, learned from the roles that were actually posted for 2026, each tagged with where it sits in the calendar and whether it is realistic for an F-1 student. treat it as a target map, not a live job board, and re-pull the live links when the season opens.

(this essay was written off the first 100-org tier and still holds — it is the strategic read. the vault has since grown to 282 orgs by adding a second, broader tier: mid-size and specialty oncology/genomics shops, diagnostics & sequencing-tool companies, cancer-center and health-system summer programs, and nonprofit institutes. the companion post **"beyond the marquee"** maps that second tier. a separate **international vault** covers non-US roles — Europe, China, Singapore/Japan/Korea, Canada/Australia.)

with that said, here is the read.

## the sectors are not equally worth your time

**pharma and big biotech (56 orgs).** this is the deepest, most structured, and most F-1-navigable pool for a cancer multi-omics PhD. genentech alone runs a whole family of computational-biology-and-medicine intern tracks — single-cell methods, spatial proteomics, statistical/functional genetics, sequence-to-function DNA models — at $50/hr in south san francisco, and the qualifications read like they were written for your CV. roche, abbvie, bms, merck, astrazeneca, j&j, takeda, sanofi all run real PhD computational/bioinformatics intern reqs. the catch worth knowing: a few pharma programs are explicitly closed to sponsorship-dependent students. **novartis's analytics PhD program states outright that CPT/OPT students do not qualify** — that is the kind of clause that quietly screens you out, and it is why this vault flags eligibility conservatively rather than optimistically.

**ML-first biotech / techbio (101 orgs).** the largest bucket by company count, the smallest by seats. insitro, recursion, tempus, generate, cellarity, xaira, noetik, immunai, and the rest each run one or two intern slots, often unstructured and lab-specific. insitro's ML intern role explicitly lists computational biology PhD students as eligible and states no visa barrier. these are excellent fits intellectually — many are doing exactly the single-cell / spatial / perturbation modeling you do — but they are lottery tickets: tiny teams, few seats, no formal program, and you usually get in through a warm intro or a paper the team already knows. worth targeting a handful, not carpet-bombing.

**big tech bio/health (11 orgs).** nvidia's bio-foundation-model intern (santa clara, building genomics/protein/human-genetics FMs) is a genuine, well-matched US role and stays open unusually late — applications were accepted at least into late february for 2026. microsoft health futures and google research run health/bio research internships. the caution: some of the most visible "bio" reqs are mislabeled or mislocated — one nvidia "bioNeMo 2026" req was actually in vietnam, and isomorphic labs (london, no internships at all) shows up constantly in searches but is a dead end for you. this vault marks those honestly so you do not waste applications.

**academic / institute / national lab (78 + 6 orgs).** broad, msk, dana-farber, fred hutch, st. jude, allen institute, nygc, flatiron institute — plus, in the second tier, a long list of cancer-center and health-system summer programs (mayo, cleveland clinic, city of hope, moffitt, mskcc, mount sinai, penn, hopkins, and university cancer centers). these host computational summer students and are scientifically a perfect fit. but two structural problems for an F-1 PhD: many are undergrad-skewed (cshl's URP, for example) or are NIH/NSF-funded programs that are citizen-only, and **the federal/DOE end is largely closed to you** — oak ridge, berkeley/JGI, argonne, PNNL, los alamos, and the NIH federal summer internship program typically require US citizenship or a green card. this vault marks every national-lab and federal-SIP role accordingly; do not spend cycles there. (read each cancer-center program's fine print — institution-funded PhD lab internships are usually fine; federally-funded SIPs usually are not.)

**diagnostics, sequencing & lab-tools (30 orgs).** a sector the first tier under-counted: natera, exact sciences, myriad, neogenomics, thermo fisher, agilent, pacbio, twist, bio-rad, plus the spatial/single-cell tool makers (vizgen, parse, scale, akoya). these run real bioinformatics/computational-genomics intern pipelines and your NGS/single-cell skills map cleanly; visa stance is mostly unstated (verify), and the work is more pipeline/clinical-genomics than discovery modeling — a solid breadth play rather than a bullseye.

## how competitive is it, really

the well-matched, well-paid, structured roles — genentech CBM, nvidia bio-FM, the brand-name pharma data-science interns — pull from the top of the quant/genomics applicant pool, and they open and fill early. the techbio startups are competitive in a different way: not volume, but fit and network. the realistic story is not "is it competitive" but "where does your specific profile convert best," and for an HRD / single-cell / spatial cancer-omics PhD the answer is clear.

## what i think you should do

1. **make pharma computational-biology your spine.** genentech (every CBM track, especially single-cell and spatial-proteomics), roche, abbvie human-genetics, bms, astrazeneca oncology data science, merck. this is where your background converts most reliably and where the F-1 path is most worn-in. apply broadly here.

2. **pick ~8 techbio shots on genuine fit, not brand.** insitro, recursion, noetik, immunai, tempus, generate, xaira, enable/vevo — the ones doing single-cell/spatial/perturbation work you can speak to fluently. lead with a paper or a method, not a generic resume.

3. **add nvidia bio-FM and microsoft/google health research as upside swings.** late deadlines, strong names, real fit if you frame the foundation-model angle.

4. **mostly skip the national labs and NIH federal SIP.** they are citizenship-gated. the exception worth a look is NIH's graduate partnerships program, which sometimes takes international students — but verify, do not assume.

5. **on immigration, default to pre-completion OPT for the summer, not CPT.** this is the single most important planning decision and it has its own playbook in this vault — read it before you accept anything, because the wrong choice can permanently cost you your post-graduation work authorization.
