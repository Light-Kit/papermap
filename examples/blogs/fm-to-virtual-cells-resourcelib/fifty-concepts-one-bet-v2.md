> *Version 2 of the [fifty-concepts tour](why-linear-baselines-win.md) — but where v1 was a neutral map that ended on an open question, this one answers it. Each numbered concept still appears once, in bold, and is carried forward without re-definition. The first twenty-eight build the shared vocabulary of the field; the reckoning is the hinge; the last twenty-two are the model [we are trying to build](what-disappears-conditional-viability-v3.md) and why a [small lab like ours](small-labs-what-to-build-v3.md) can build it. After this essay, the [model essay](what-disappears-conditional-viability-v3.md), its [engineering breakdown](the-math-of-the-survival-field-v2.md), and the [school it bets against](distributional-generation-vs-survival-field.md) read as the obvious next three coordinates.*

## Part I — The shared map

### The substrate: what a virtual cell foundation model eats

A **(1) foundation model (FM)** is a high-capacity neural network pretrained on a large self-supervised corpus and then adapted to many downstream tasks; the lineage is the language-model wave that produced GPT and BERT. A **(2) virtual cell (VC)** is the artifact the single-cell community wants on the other end — a computational object you can ask "what would this cell look like if I gave it drug X or knocked out gene Y" and get back a wet-lab-replicable answer. The base material is **(3) single-cell atlases** — millions of observed transcriptomes organized into resources like **(4) Tabula Sapiens** and the Human Cell Atlas. These are observational catalogs of which states cells happen to occupy, not records of what happens when you reach in. To get intervention data you need **(5) Perturb-seq** ([Replogle et al., *Cell* 2022](https://www.cell.com/cell/fulltext/S0092-8674(22)00746-8)): single-cell RNA-seq paired with CRISPR edits, so each cell carries a known perturbation tag. The largest perturbation atlas as of 2026 is **(6) Tahoe-100M** ([Vevo + Arc, 2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.02.20.639398v3.full)) — a hundred million cells across 1,100 drugs and 50 cancer lines, dose-resolved. The whole field rests on one distinction the rest of this essay will keep returning to: **(7) observational vs interventional data** — atlases tell you what co-occurs, perturbation atlases tell you what an intervention does, and the gap between them is where every hard problem lives.

### Models, architectures, and the adapters bolted on

The 2023–2024 single-cell FM wave produced five anchor models. **(8) scGPT** ([Cui et al., *Nat Methods* 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-024-02201-0)) is generative, 51M params, trained on 33M cells. **(9) Geneformer** ([Theodoris et al., *Nature* 2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06139-9)) is encoder-only, masked-token, 10–316M params across the cancer-curated checkpoints. **(10) scFoundation** is an xTrimoGene encoder with read-depth-aware attention — the closest existing sc-FM to architecture–biology co-design. **(11) UCE** is ViT-style, 650M params, cross-species. **(12) CellPLM** is the cell-as-token variant. All five lean on **(13) masked-gene modeling** as the pretraining loss — the NLP playbook transposed to gene tokens via **(14) tokenization** schemes that map expression counts to a finite vocabulary. **(15) STATE** ([Arc Institute, 2025](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.06.26.661135)) shipped as a perturbation-specialist FM pretrained on ~170M unperturbed and ~100M perturbed cells — the substrate-as-moat answer to the masked-token ceiling. **(16) scVI** ([Lopez et al., *Nat Methods* 2018](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-018-0229-2)) sits underneath the whole stack as the variational autoencoder whose ELBO is still the cleanest per-cell uncertainty handle in production.

When fine-tuning beats pretraining, the bottleneck moves to the head. An **(17) adapter** — LoRA, FiLM, or bias modulation — is a small (<1% of params) trainable module that specializes a **(18) frozen backbone** for a downstream task without retraining the substrate. The 2026 architectural counter-move is **(19) xVERSE**, a transcriptomics-native, non-LM architecture that beats LM-pedigree FMs on representation and imputation — the first published evidence the transformer shape is not load-bearing. Hold onto the adapter and the frozen backbone: they are also the reason a small lab can play this game at all.

### The reckoning, and the opening hiding inside it

In August 2025 **(20) Ahlmann-Eltze & Huber** ([*Nat Methods* 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-025-02772-6)) showed that a trivial **(21) linear baseline** — predict the mean of training perturbations, no FM, no fine-tune — matched or beat every published single-cell FM on perturbation prediction. The follow-up wave established the **(22) latent-additive baseline** (a one-line regression on FM embeddings) as the new floor and decomposed the failure into four causes: wrong loss, wrong architecture, leaky splits, and the finding that the FM had learned cell-type identity rather than regulatory logic. The cleanest theoretical name is **(23) causal transportability** in Pearl's sense — a model fit on P(X | do(Y), Z=z₁) does not predict under Z=z₂ without structural assumptions — which maps onto **(24) Pearl's ladder of causation**: rung 1 association, rung 2 intervention, rung 3 counterfactual. Atlases are rung-1; perturbation prediction is rung-2; the gap explains everything. The field's response is **(25) in silico perturbation (ISP)** — pursued as better data, as perturbation-aware adapters, or as **(26) closed-loop ISP** orchestrated by **(27) LLM agents** that pick the next experiment. (For the full anatomy of why the baselines win, see [why linear baselines win](why-linear-baselines-win.md).)

Here the v1 tour stopped, on a shrug: every essay asks what it would take to close the gap. This version refuses the shrug. Look at *which* models lose. Almost all of them — CPA, chemCPA, CellFlow, and the diffusion entrants like Squidiff — are playing the same game: **(28) expression-of-survivors**, predicting what the treated population looks like, mass-conserving, no death. That is a single crowded axis, and it is the one losing to a ridge regression ([the full argument is the distributional-generation essay](distributional-generation-vs-survival-field.md)). A crowded axis that is losing is not a closed field. It is a signpost pointing at the axis nobody is on.

## Part II — The bet

### The object we are trying to build

The axis nobody is on is the question the crowded models structurally cannot answer: not *what do the survivors look like* but **(29) who survived** — a conditional survival field π(*x* | drug *g*, dose *d*), the probability a cell at state *x* persists under that treatment. We make it concrete as an **(30) integrated hazard collapsed to a snapshot**: each cell carries a death hazard λ, and over the fixed Tahoe window τ the surviving fraction is π = exp(−τ·λ). The engine for that hazard is borrowed wholesale from outside biology: **(31) reliability engineering**, specifically **(31, cont.) accelerated life testing** ([Nelson](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/book/10.1002/9780470316795)), where you stress a part and fit a hazard with a parametric acceleration law chosen so it *extrapolates* to stresses you never tested. Map dose to stress and you get **(32) dose-as-stress**: an inverse-power-law acceleration log λ = log λ₀(*z*) + *s*·log *d* + *b*. Two properties fall out for free — **(33) dose-monotonicity**, guaranteed by a monotone neural network ([UMNN](https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.05164)) rather than hoped for, and extrapolation to unseen dose, the entire point of an accelerated-life model. The biology already knew the scalar version: **(34) the radiobiology linear-quadratic curve**, surviving fraction = exp(−αD−βD²), is exactly this object with state and drug integrated out; we are restoring the resolution it throws away. The drug enters through a frozen **(35) MoLFormer** ([Ross et al., 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.09553)) chemical embedding *c_g* — the move that lets π predict for a molecule never trained on — and the cell through the frozen **(36) Tahoe-x1** ([2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.10.23.683759.full.pdf)) latent vector, so the model never relearns what a cell is.

### The loss, and the clauses that keep it honest

We never observe a death; we observe two compositional densities per well, control and treated. The generative story — kill each control cell with probability 1−π, then let survivors drift — is exactly the **(37) unbalanced optimal transport** ([Chizat et al., 2018](https://arxiv.org/abs/1508.05216)) decomposition of a coupling into a reaction term and a transport term, so the objective is a single entropic-UOT divergence in which **(38) the Wasserstein–Fisher–Rao split** ([Chizat et al., 2015](https://arxiv.org/abs/1506.06430)) does the work: the mass-destruction component *is* π, and the transport component *is* the state transition. This is precisely why **(39) Waddington-OT and moscot** ([Schiebinger et al., 2019](https://www.cell.com/cell/fulltext/S0092-8674(19)30039-X); [Klein et al., *Nature* 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08453-2)) are our **baselines, not our engine**: they are the only single-cell methods that already split killed from transported mass, but they fit a fresh coupling per condition and so cannot predict an unseen drug — we learn the split as a function of (*g*, *d*) instead. The transport branch itself is modern neural transport, and crucially its **(40) growth-aware / unbalanced** variants — [UDSB](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.09099) (shown on drug-induced death), [CytoBridge](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.11197), [Joint Velocity-Growth Flow Matching](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.13413) — whose growth head *is* the death field, unifying the two branches.

Two clauses keep the whole thing from lying. Because treated counts are compositional, π is identified only up to a per-condition constant — **(41) compositional identifiability**, the same constant ecology calls occupancy and PU-learning calls the [Elkan–Noto](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1401890.1401920) labeling constant — so an **(42) absolute-count anchor** (spike-ins, per-well counts) or a low-dose calibration prior is load-bearing, and we say so in the paper. And because not all disappearance is death, **(43) competing risks** separates true killing from non-lethal dropout (cell-cycle arrest, doublet loss), which the death-vs-transport split is meant to absorb only if the transport map is expressive enough to claim arrested cells as *moved*. The claim then stands or falls on **(44) the falsification battery**: held-out molecules (the load-bearing test WOT/moscot structurally cannot attempt), held-out doses (a direct test of the acceleration law), and correlation with BH3-profiling apoptotic priming ([Sarosiek et al., *Cancer Cell* 2017](https://www.cell.com/cancer-cell/fulltext/S1535-6108(17)30048-9)) — an orthogonal assay, so agreement is not circular. (The full module-by-module construction is [the math primer](the-math-of-the-survival-field-v2.md).)

## Part III — Why a small lab like us

The reason this is a direction *we* can take, and not only Arc or DeepMind, is the shape of its cost. **(45) Frozen-encoder economics**: because Tahoe-x1 and MoLFormer are frozen, we pay no pretraining bill — we train only λ₀, the acceleration heads, and the transport map, the small-lab winning pattern the [whole arc keeps landing on](small-labs-what-to-build-v3.md). **(46) Transferred math**: the inductive biases — survival = exp(−H), the WFR death/transport split, dose-as-stress monotonicity, identifiability-up-to-a-constant — are *borrowed* from reliability engineering, optimal transport, and survival analysis, not invented; what is new is pointing them at one cell-biology question, and that is exactly the kind of novelty that does not require a compute moat. **(47) A public substrate**: Tahoe-100M is open, so the data advantage that usually decides these races is neutralized. **(48) The open axis**: the crowdedness of the expression-of-survivors school is the evidence that *who-survived* is uncontested — the rare case where the highest-leverage question is also the least-worked one. **(49) The falsifiable prelim**: the first version is a two-week, single-A100 experiment with binary falsifiers, in the same mold as [the niche-dependence prelim](reading-niche-dependence-off-frozen-cell-fms.md) — small enough to run, sharp enough to kill. And **(50) reckoning-grade evaluation as the moat**: from the first experiment we hold ourselves to a tuned linear baseline, held-out-molecule and held-out-dose splits, and distributional metrics — because the lesson of the reckoning is that the discipline, as much as the object, is what separates a real result from a beautiful one.

Fifty concepts, one bet. The first twenty-eight are the field as everyone inherits it; the reckoning is the door; and the last twenty-two are a model whose mathematics is borrowed on purpose, whose substrate is public, whose evaluation is honest by construction, and whose one new idea — predict *who survives* a drug you have never run — is small enough for a small lab and open enough to be worth the run.
