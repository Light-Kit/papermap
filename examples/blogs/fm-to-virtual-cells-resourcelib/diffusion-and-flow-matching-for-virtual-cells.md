> *A methods companion to the reachability program. The [cell-state reachability essay](cell-state-reachability-as-viability-theory.md) proposes a model built on a diffusion / flow-matching backbone; this essay explains what that backbone is, why generative models took over virtual cells, and how the named systems work. Cross-cuts to [reachability and forbidden states](reachability-and-forbidden-states-in-cell-fms.md) on what generation cannot yet do, [stability](cell-stability-and-niche-dependence-in-vc-fms.md) on the dynamical-systems framing, and the [fifty-concepts essay](fifty-concepts-one-bet-v2.md) for the surrounding vocabulary.*

## Why generative models at all

The first wave of single-cell foundation models — scGPT, Geneformer, scFoundation — were trained to *embed and classify*: turn a cell into a vector, then read cell type, batch, or a perturbation label off that vector. That answers "what is this cell." It does not answer "what cells could exist," and the latter is the question virtual-cell work actually cares about. A generative model learns the probability distribution over cell states rather than a decision boundary on top of it, which buys three things at once: you can *sample* new plausible cells, you can ask how *likely* any proposed cell is (the density itself), and you can *condition* generation on a perturbation, cell type, or niche and read the response. The masked-token objective never learned a usable density; diffusion and flow matching do. That shift — from discriminative embedding to learned density — is the generative turn, and it is why the 2024–2026 virtual-cell frontier is built on these two families rather than on bigger BERTs.

## Diffusion in one paragraph

A diffusion model defines a *forward* process that gradually adds Gaussian noise to a real data point until, after many steps, it is indistinguishable from pure noise, and then learns the *reverse* process that denoises noise back into data ([Ho, Jain & Abbeel, NeurIPS 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239); [Song et al., ICLR 2021](https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.13456)). The thing the network actually learns is the **score** — the gradient of the log-density, ∇ log p(x) — at every noise level ([Song & Ermon, NeurIPS 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/1907.05600)). That detail matters for biology: the score points "uphill" toward the data manifold from wherever you are, so a trained diffusion model is implicitly a map of how far any point sits from the realized distribution and which direction climbs back onto it. Sampling means starting from noise and following the learned reverse dynamics, a stochastic differential equation, down onto the manifold. The cost is that sampling takes many network evaluations.

## Flow matching in one paragraph

Flow matching ([Lipman et al., ICLR 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.02747); related stochastic interpolants, [Albergo & Vanden-Eijnden, ICLR 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.15571)) keeps the noise-to-data idea but learns a **velocity field** instead of a score: a vector at every point and time that says which way to move to transport a simple base distribution (Gaussian noise) into the data distribution along a chosen probability path. Generation is then solving an ordinary differential equation — follow the velocity field from noise to data — which is deterministic and needs far fewer steps than the diffusion SDE, so inference is faster. Training is also simpler: you regress the network onto a closed-form target velocity along straight-line interpolants between noise and data, with none of the noise-schedule bookkeeping diffusion needs. The two families are close cousins — both transport noise to data, one via a score-driven SDE, one via a velocity-driven ODE — and in practice flow matching has become the default when the generator must run inside an active-learning loop where inference speed is the bottleneck.

## How they land on cells

The same machinery, pointed at gene-expression vectors instead of images. **scDiffusion** ([Luo et al., *Bioinformatics* 2024](https://academic.oup.com/bioinformatics/article/40/9/btae518/7738782)) was the first big conditional diffusion model for scRNA-seq, generating realistic cells and interpolating trajectories with classifier guidance. **CFGen** ([Palma et al., ICLR 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.11734)) is a multi-modal, multi-attribute conditional flow-matching generator with a count-aware likelihood, so it respects the discrete, over-dispersed structure of real scRNA-seq counts rather than treating expression as Gaussian — the cleanest "generate a cell conditioned on X" tool currently available. **CellFlow** ([Klein, Tejada-Lapuerta, Theis & Bunne, bioRxiv 2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.04.11.648220v1)) uses flow matching to predict whole-population perturbation responses and run in-silico combinatorial morphogen screens. **scDiffEq** ([Cofer et al., bioRxiv 2023](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.12.06.570508)) fits a neural stochastic differential equation — drift plus diffusion — directly to LARRY lineage-traced data, making the diffusion *the dynamics* rather than just a sampler. Around them sit the optimal-transport cousins that solve the same transport problem by a different route: Waddington-OT ([Schiebinger et al., *Cell* 2019](https://www.cell.com/cell/fulltext/S0092-8674(19)30039-X)), CellOT and Schrödinger-bridge couplings ([Bunne et al., *Nat Methods* 2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-023-01969-x)), and manifold-constrained flows like MIOFlow ([Huguet et al., NeurIPS 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.14928)). The VAE generation of single-cell models (scVI, [Lopez et al., *Nat Methods* 2018](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-018-0229-2)) is the predecessor these displaced: same generative goal, blurrier samples, looser density.

## Why the fit is so natural

Four properties make these models unusually well-matched to virtual-cell questions. First, the **learned density is the manifold of realized states** — a high-likelihood region is exactly "cells like the ones biology produces," which is the object every downstream reachability or stability question is defined against. Second, **classifier-free guidance** turns conditioning into a dial: train with and without a label, then at sampling time push generation toward "IL-7-dependent," "post-perturbation," or "Treg" by interpolating the two — the niche and perturbation knobs come for free. Third, the **score points back onto the manifold**, so the same trained model that generates cells also scores how off-manifold a proposed cell is and in which direction it would have to move to become plausible — the seed of an out-of-distribution and, eventually, a forbidden-state signal. Fourth, **diffusion bridges and OT couplings interpolate between two cell states**, which is precisely a developmental or reprogramming trajectory: a Schrödinger bridge from a fibroblast distribution to an iPSC distribution is a candidate cocktail path, not just a pretty interpolation.

## The honest limits, and where this goes

Three caveats keep the generative turn from being oversold. **Density is not causality** — a model that knows IL-7-program cells and TGFβ-program cells co-occur in the right proportions has learned the joint distribution, not the regulatory mechanism, exactly the regulatory-logic gap the [interpretability essay](interpretability-state-of-cell-fms.md) documents. **Sample quality is not biological correctness** — a generated cell can pass every distributional check and still be a profile no real cell would adopt, because the model only ever saw realized data and has no notion of what is *impossible*. And **nothing in standard diffusion or flow matching says which states are forbidden** — low likelihood conflates "rare" with "cannot exist," and separating those is the entire job the [viability-theory reachability model](cell-state-reachability-as-viability-theory.md) takes on by adding a stratum head and a physics-informed forbidden energy on top of exactly this backbone. That is the throughline: diffusion and flow matching give the field its first usable density over cell state; the next move is to carve that density into what is realized, what is engineerable, and what nature rules out. The generative engine is built — the question is what you bolt onto it.
