> *Eighth essay in the epistemic-honesty arc, and the first that stops asking what the existing FMs cannot do and starts proposing the model that would. The prior seven — [surprise](surprise-and-uncertainty-in-cell-fms.md), [reachability](reachability-and-forbidden-states-in-cell-fms.md), [translatability](translatability-dual-latent-vc-fm.md), [stability](cell-stability-and-niche-dependence-in-vc-fms.md), [multimodal-stability](multimodal-stability-and-failure-modes-in-vc-fms.md), [interpretability state-of-field](interpretability-state-of-cell-fms.md), and the [niche-dependence prelim](reading-niche-dependence-off-frozen-cell-fms.md) — diagnose the gap and ship the smallest experiment that fills one piece of it. The [fifty-concepts essay](fifty-concepts-one-essay.md) consolidates the vocabulary. This essay assembles the pieces into a research program: a stratified-manifold foundation model for what can exist, what can be made, and what cannot.*

## The question

The first seven essays converged on a question the field has been asking around rather than at: not "how accurately can we predict perturbation response" but "what states can a cell occupy at all?" The reachability sibling sketched the answer as natural / induced / forbidden but stopped at the diagnosis. The stability sibling decomposed `stable` into four interpretable heads, and the niche-dependence prelim ships the one new head (λ) as a two-week falsifier. This essay names the model the prelim is the first step toward — a *reachability foundation model* that classifies any query cell state into three strata, attributes the classification to a named cause, and proposes a path when the state is reachable. The mathematical backbone is forty years old but has never been applied at FM scale to single cells. The model recipe is a thin layer on top of existing diffusion / flow-matching machinery. The novelty is the combination, and the agent's task is to build the v0.1.

## The theoretical backbone

The right citation for the framing is not out-of-distribution detection or open-set recognition — it is Aubin's viability theory ([Aubin, *Viability Theory*, Birkhäuser 1991](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-642-16684-6); revised [Aubin, Bayen & Saint-Pierre, 2011](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-642-16684-6)), the mathematics of which states a constrained dynamical system can occupy and how it gets there. Three native objects map directly onto the three cell-state strata: the **viability kernel** (states reachable under the natural dynamics, indefinitely) is the realized interior; the **capture basin** (states reachable under controlled inputs that drive the system back into the viability kernel) is the engineerable boundary; the **non-viable complement** is the forbidden exterior. Hamilton-Jacobi reachability ([Mitchell, Bayen & Tomlin, *IEEE TAC* 2005](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/1463302); [Bansal, Chen, Herbert & Tomlin, CDC 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1709.07523)) gives the computational machinery, and a recent biology paper ([Hirsch & Herbert, arXiv 2503.11021, 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.11021)) demonstrates that HJ reachability ports to two-timescale gene-regulatory networks at small scale. No one has carried the framework to atlas-scale cell foundation models. The pitch becomes a single line: *viability theory operationalised on a cell foundation model.* That sentence is what makes the rest of the program defensible against the reviewer who reflexively reaches for "this is just OOD detection."

## The model framework

The backbone is borrowed. CFGen ([Palma et al., ICLR 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.11734)), CellFlow ([Klein, Tejada-Lapuerta, Theis & Bunne, bioRxiv 2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.04.11.648220v1)), or scDiffusion ([Luo et al., *Bioinformatics* 2024](https://academic.oup.com/bioinformatics/article/40/9/btae518/7738782)) all give a usable density on the cell-state manifold via flow matching ([Lipman et al., ICLR 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.02747)) or denoising diffusion ([Ho et al., NeurIPS 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239)). The contribution sits in three heads added on top.

**Stratum head.** A three-way classifier with calibrated confidence — realized / engineerable / forbidden — supervised end-to-end and treated formally as a partitioned open-set-recognition problem ([Scheirer et al., *IEEE TPAMI* 2013](https://www.wjscheirer.com/papers/wjs_tpami2013_openset.pdf)). Conformal prediction ([Vovk, Gammerman & Shafer, 2005](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/b106715)) gives per-prediction confidence intervals, which is what carries the falsifiability claim through review.

**Connectivity prior.** A graph-Laplacian regularizer over lineage-traced trees (LARRY, [Weinreb et al., *Science* 2020](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.aaw3381); Cospar; CARLIN). Every realized state has a continuous high-density path back to an ancestor, by *omnis cellula e cellula*. This generalizes scDiffEq ([Cofer et al., bioRxiv 2023](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.12.06.570508)), which uses LARRY barcodes for supervised fate labels — we use them as a connectivity penalty in training instead. The strict-generalization framing is what survives a reviewer who has read scDiffEq.

**Forbidden-energy term.** A summed energy that spikes off the realized + engineerable manifold, with three named contributors so the energy is causally attributable rather than a black-box score. (i) Regulatory antagonism scored from SCENIC+ GRNs ([Bravo González-Blas et al., *Nat Methods* 2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-023-01938-4)) penalizes mutually-antagonistic master regulators co-expressed at saturation. (ii) Regulatory FBA infeasibility ([van Berlo et al., *IEEE/ACM TCBB* 2011](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/5560791); FlexFlux, [Marmiesse, Peyraud & Cottret, *BMC Syst Biol* 2015](https://bmcsystbiol.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s12918-015-0238-z)) extends classical FBA infeasibility detection from metabolic flux to regulatory-state vectors. (iii) Boolean regulatory-logic compatibility against curated motif rules from the cell-decision literature. The manifold-EBM and MPDR literature ([Du & Mordatch, NeurIPS 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.08689); [Yoon et al., NeurIPS 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.18677)) gives the formal scaffolding for an energy that lives off the data manifold — without it the forbidden head reads as ad hoc.

**Synthetic-negative generator.** The forbidden class has no atlas; we construct it. Sample antagonistic TF pairs from SCENIC+, set both at saturation, embed. Mix incompatible identity programs at saturation (Foxp3 with T-bet, Hb with myosin). Inject Gaussian noise outside the data support. This generator is the most reproducibility-critical piece of the entire system and should ship as a standalone module with unit tests, not as an inline helper.

**Reachability proposer.** Given a query target in the engineerable stratum, a Schrödinger bridge ([Bunne et al., *Nat Methods* 2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-023-01969-x)) between a somatic source and the target proposes the optimal continuous path, and the path's TF-trajectory becomes the cocktail proposal. Yamanaka-style discovery becomes the natural wet-lab-anchored validation: does the proposer rediscover OCT4 / SOX2 / KLF4 / MYC when asked for a fibroblast → iPSC path on data it has not seen?

## What is genuinely ours, in honest accounting

The diffusion backbone is borrowed (CFGen / CellFlow). The lineage-aware training signal is a strict generalization of scDiffEq. The viability-theory framing is a port from control theory, never operationalized on a cell FM. The forbidden-energy fusion (SCENIC+ regulatory antagonism + rFBA flux infeasibility + Boolean regulatory-logic compatibility + manifold-EBM scaffolding) is genuinely new — no preprint or paper to date combines these terms on a cell-foundation-model embedding. The stratum head as causal-attribution open-set recognition — three classes, each with a *named* contributing cause — is a new framing on top of vanilla OOD detection. The combination is the contribution. Viability theory is what makes the combination feel inevitable rather than improvised, and that is the citation that will defend the paper at review.

## The v0.1 we should build

Concrete two-month scope. One A100 node, roughly 150 GPU-hours, three pre-registered falsifiers, one repository.

**Backbone.** Frozen CFGen checkpoint on Tabula Sapiens v2 plus iPSC reprogramming time-courses ([Hanna et al., *Nature* 2009](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature08137); recent Yamanaka panels in CELLxGENE Census). Do not retrain; the prelim discipline carries.

**Heads.** Small MLPs on top of the CFGen residual stream. Total parameters added under 5% of backbone. Train end-to-end with frozen backbone for roughly one week.

**Forbidden energy, staged.** Start with SCENIC+ regulatory antagonism only; bring rFBA in as v0.2, Boolean logic as v0.3. The same scope discipline that bound the niche-dependence prelim binds this one — one new term per release.

**Three pre-registered falsifiers, written into `PROTOCOL.md` before any result.**

1. **Stratum macro-AUROC > 0.80** on the three-way contrast (Tabula Sapiens realized cells vs Hanna iPSC reprogramming intermediates vs synthetic-negative generator output), bootstrap CI, held-out test split with explicit donor-level leakage controls.
2. **Yamanaka recovery.** Given a fibroblast source and an iPSC-resembling target embedded in the engineerable stratum, the Schrödinger-bridge cocktail proposer ranks OCT4 / SOX2 / KLF4 / MYC in the top-five TF candidates by integrated trajectory weight. This is the wet-lab-anchored falsifier the [interpretability sibling](interpretability-state-of-cell-fms.md) named as the missing piece across the cell-FM literature.
3. **Forbidden-energy null.** Synthetic-negative cells have higher forbidden energy than realized atlas cells with bootstrap CI not crossing zero, and random gene-set ablations on real atlas cells do *not* move the energy outside the realized band. Without this control the forbidden head is decoration.

If all three pass, the substrate is real and the paper writes itself. If any fails, the paper becomes a calibrated negative result — what the cell-FM field needs as badly as the positive one.

## Verdict and the agent's task

This is the model the niche-dependence prelim is a step toward. The prelim earns the credibility (two falsifiers, two weeks, scGPT-only) to be heard on the larger claim (viability theory + stratified manifold + forbidden energy). The work is sequenced in three commits the downstream agent can pick up. (i) Draft `PROTOCOL.md` — pre-registered falsifiers and synthetic-negative-generator spec, mirroring the `niche-lambda` discipline. (ii) Build the v0.1 — CFGen fine-tune + three heads + the three falsifiers, single A100 over two months. (iii) Ship the repo as `strata/` or `cellreach/` with the forbidden-energy module as the defended IP and the synthetic-negative generator as the most-tested file. Three risks to pre-empt: reviewer-2 will say *this is just OOD detection* (counter with causal-attribution OSR + viability-theory framing in the abstract); scDiffEq already uses LARRY (frame the connectivity prior as a strict generalization, not an invention); the synthetic-negative generator is the credibility load-bearing piece (ship as a standalone module with unit tests, not buried in the training loop). Names to follow for being scooped by: **Charlotte Bunne** (EPFL/Stanford, closest in spirit — CellFlow + CellOT); **Fabian Theis** (Helmholtz Munich, owns the CFGen pipeline); **Sylvia Herbert** (UCSD, the only person already applying HJ reachability to biological networks). A virtual cell that cannot tell you what cannot exist is a virtual cell that has not yet earned its name. The next move is to build one that can.
