> *Seventh essay in the epistemic-honesty arc. The first six asked what the model does not know ([surprise](surprise-and-uncertainty-in-cell-fms.md)), cannot transfer ([translatability](translatability-dual-latent-vc-fm.md)), cannot reach ([reachability](reachability-and-forbidden-states-in-cell-fms.md)), cannot sustain ([stability](cell-stability-and-niche-dependence-in-vc-fms.md)), cannot cross-validate across modalities ([multimodal-stability](multimodal-stability-and-failure-modes-in-vc-fms.md)), and what the interpretability community has actually read off it so far ([interpretability state-of-field](interpretability-state-of-cell-fms.md)). This one stops asking and ships. It is the prelim version of the λ head in the [stability sibling](cell-stability-and-niche-dependence-in-vc-fms.md), scoped to fourteen days and bound by two falsifiers. Cross-cuts to [the closed-loop 101](closed-loop-virtual-cells-101.md) on what a downstream agent does with the answer.*

## The question

The stability essay sketched four interpretable heads on top of a frozen single-cell foundation model — basin depth U, niche dependence λ, lifespan τ, population frequency π — and named them as the four axes the current FM stack does not expose. Of the four, three are reductions to existing machinery. PRESCIENT ([Yeo et al., *Nat Commun* 2021](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-021-23518-w)) gives U; SenMayo ([Saul et al., *Nat Commun* 2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-022-32552-1)) and apoptosis-priming gene signatures ([Sarosiek et al., *Cancer Cell* 2017](https://www.cell.com/cancer-cell/fulltext/S1535-6108(17)30048-9)) cover τ in pieces; π is atlas marginal density that scVI ([Lopez et al., *Nat Methods* 2018](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-018-0229-2)) and CellxGene already serve. Only λ is genuinely missing — no published cell-FM ships a per-cell, per-ligand niche-dependence vector with a falsifier attached. The question of this essay is what the smallest version of λ that anyone can run on a frozen public FM in two weeks looks like, and what binary tests it has to pass before anybody is allowed to call it a contribution.

## Why this wedge, scoped this hard

The constraint that makes this prelim honest is refusal. U / τ / π get cut. Multi-FM benchmarks get cut. Fine-tuning gets cut. New pretraining gets cut. End-to-end training of any adapter gets cut. What is left is a forward-pass-only probe of an existing frozen checkpoint — scGPT human-pretrained ([Cui et al., *Nat Methods* 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-024-02201-0)), Geneformer-V2 ([Theodoris et al., *Nature* 2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06139-9)) as fallback — running on cells from Tabula Sapiens v2 ([CELLxGENE Census](https://chanzuckerberg.github.io/cellxgene-census/)) and a single 10x Visium lymph-node slide. The discipline is that the talk on day fourteen has to defend exactly one claim: a frozen FM contains a niche-dependence signal that can be read off by responder-set ablation and validated against two independent ground truths. Not "the FM is interpretable." Not "the FM solves Perturb-seq." One claim. Two falsifiers. The reckoning literature ([Ahlmann-Eltze & Huber, *Nat Methods* 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-025-02692-8)) earned its credibility by holding the question at that scale; the prelim has to as well.

## The method, in one paragraph

Pick five ligands well-covered by the NicheNet ligand-target prior ([Browaeys et al., *Nat Methods* 2020](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-019-0667-5)) — IL-7, TGFβ, IL-2, IFNγ, TNFα. For each cell c and each ligand L, look up the responder gene-set R(L) (the top ~200 NicheNet-v2-weighted downstream targets). Compute the cell's frozen-FM embedding z(c). Construct a perturbed input by masking every gene in R(L) to the population median; recompute z(c'_L). Define λ(c, L) as the L₂ distance ‖z(c) − z(c'_L)‖ — or its cosine ablation form 1 − cos(z(c), z(c'_L)). Output is a five-dimensional per-cell vector. The biological claim is that if a cell sits on the IL-7 program, masking the IL-7 responder set should perturb its embedding more than masking a random gene-set of the same size; the vector entries then name the cell's niche dependencies in language a wet-lab immunologist already speaks. No architectural change. No fine-tune. No new pretraining run. The entire contribution is the choice of probe and the discipline of the falsifiers.

## The falsifiers — both binary, both must pass

**Falsifier 1, known-pair separability.** Pull naive CD4 T cells and regulatory T cells from Tabula Sapiens immune subset. The textbook biology is that naive CD4 cells require tonic IL-7 ([Tan et al., *PNAS* 2001](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.161126098)) and Tregs require TGFβ for Foxp3 maintenance ([Chen et al., *J Exp Med* 2003](https://rupress.org/jem/article/198/12/1875/52221)). The pass criterion is AUROC > 0.75 on "is this cell a Treg" using the ratio λ_TGFβ / λ_IL-7 as the score. If the AUROC falls below 0.6 on a 5k-cell prototype run on day one, the substrate does not carry niche-program granularity at the final layer and the prelim escalates immediately — mid-layer probe, Geneformer-V2 fallback, then negative-result paper. This is the gate. It costs three hours of compute and it answers the entire question of whether the project is real.

**Falsifier 2, spatial-neighbor ligand exposure.** On a 10x Visium human lymph-node slide, deconvolve cell composition per spot with cell2location ([Kleshchevnikov et al., *Nat Biotechnol* 2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-021-01139-4)) or RCTD ([Cable et al., *Nat Biotechnol* 2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-021-00830-w)). For each spot, compute per-cell λ via the same pipeline, and separately compute NicheNet-inferred ligand exposure from physical neighbors. The pass criterion is Spearman ρ > 0.3 between predicted λ_L and neighbor-inferred ligand exposure for at least three of the five ligands. ρ rather than r because the signal is rank-order, and 0.3 rather than 0.5 because Visium deconvolution noise on a single slide is large.

**Negative controls, all must show null.** Sample ten random gene-sets of the same size as R(L), recompute λ on the naive-versus-Treg contrast; the random-set λ distribution must overlap. Scramble cell-to-dominant-ligand pairings; AUROC must collapse to ~0.5. Shuffle cell-type labels; the Treg-versus-naive contrast must vanish. Without this battery the demo is decoration.

## What could kill the prelim, and what to do about it

The honest worry comes from the [interpretability state-of-field sibling](interpretability-state-of-cell-fms.md). The 2025–2026 mechanistic-interpretability wave on cell FMs ([Pedrocchi, Barkmann, Joudaki & Boeva, bioRxiv Oct 2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.10.22.681631v2), accepted ICLR 2026; [Kendiukhov, arXiv 2603.02952](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02952)) found that only roughly six to ten percent of transcription factors in scGPT and Geneformer-V2 produce features with regulatory-target-specific responses — the substrate carries co-occurring biology, not causal regulators. Ligand-program features may be similarly sparse. The mitigation is structural: the day-one prototype is precisely the cheap test for this. If naive-versus-Treg separability fails at the final layer, the layer-stratified atlas ([Kendiukhov, arXiv 2603.02952](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02952)) says mid-layer features may carry pathway content more cleanly than the final layer — the day-three escalation is to repeat the probe on layer-four or layer-six embeddings. If that also fails, the prelim flips: it becomes the first quantitative report of "responder-set ablation does not recover known niche dependence on scGPT or Geneformer," with a layer-stratified table and a recommendation about what the next FM needs to do differently. That paper is also worth shipping. A two-falsifier prelim that fails honestly is more useful to the field than a thirty-figure prelim that quietly elides its negative results.

## What the demo shows on day fourteen

Four figures. Figure one: a violin plot of λ_IL-7 versus λ_TGFβ across naive CD4, Treg, and memory CD4, with an ROC curve for the Treg-versus-naive contrast and the AUROC printed in the corner. Figure two: a Visium-spot scatter of predicted λ_L versus NicheNet-neighbor ligand exposure, faceted by ligand, with per-panel Spearman ρ. Figure three: the negative-control panel — random-responder λ overlaid on real-responder λ, demonstrating that the signal is ligand-specific rather than any-perturbation-moves-the-embedding. Figure four, the stretch: λ_TGFβ on tumor-infiltrating versus peripheral-blood Tregs from the Sade-Feldman anti-PD1 melanoma cohort ([Sade-Feldman et al., *Cell* 2018](https://www.cell.com/cell/fulltext/S0092-8674(18)31568-X), GSE120575), a clinically-anchored prediction that doesn't have to pass for the demo but is the headline if it does. The talk closes by linking the prelim back to the four-head program: λ is the smallest of the four heads that contains the project's novelty, the substrate either supports it or doesn't, and the answer is now empirical rather than rhetorical. The next sibling — U head via PRESCIENT-on-scGPT, or τ head via SenMayo-supervised regression — is decided after week two, not before. A virtual cell that cannot tell you which ligand it lives on is not a virtual cell yet, and the field gets to know in fourteen days whether the existing FMs are even a candidate substrate for the question.
