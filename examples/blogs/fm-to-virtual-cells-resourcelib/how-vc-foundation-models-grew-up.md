> *A field-history companion to the [fifty-concepts bet](fifty-concepts-one-bet-v2.md): same device — fifty numbered concepts, each in bold, carried once — but where that essay argues for one model, this one maps the whole arc. Four acts, each a verb the field learned in order. The deep-dives live elsewhere: the [reckoning](why-linear-baselines-win.md), the [diffusion/flow explainer](diffusion-and-flow-matching-for-virtual-cells.md) and [how transformers and diffusion compose](transformers-and-diffusion-together-in-vc.md), the [causal-models survey](causal-models-fm-and-vc.md), the [interpretability state-of-play](interpretability-state-of-cell-fms.md), and the [survival-field model](what-disappears-conditional-viability-v3.md) this arc points toward.*

The history of virtual-cell foundation models is a ladder of ambition — a record of what we successively dared to ask a cell to tell us. Four rungs, four verbs. First we asked it to hold still and be *represented*; then we *reckoned* with how little that representation actually predicted; then we taught models to *generate* whole cells rather than label them; and now we are asking them to find the *cause* — and, at the top of the ladder, to *intervene*. Fifty concepts, in order, carried once each.

## Act I — REPRESENT: give a cell coordinates

A **(1) foundation model (FM)** is a high-capacity network pretrained once on a large unlabeled corpus and then adapted to many tasks; the **(2) virtual cell (VC)** is the prize at the end — a computational cell you can query with "what if." The first move was to borrow the language-model recipe: **(3) self-supervised pretraining** on millions of transcriptomes, with **(4) tokenization** turning a cell's expression counts into a finite vocabulary the network can attend over, trained by **(5) masked-gene modeling** — hide some genes, predict them, learn a representation as a side effect.

The opening shot was **(6) scBERT** ([Yang et al., *Nat Mach Intell* 2022](https://doi.org/10.1038/s42256-022-00534-z)), BERT transposed onto genes. Then the wave: **(7) Geneformer** ([Theodoris et al., *Nature* 2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06139-9)) encoded each cell as a *rank* ordering of its genes; **(8) scGPT** ([Cui et al., *Nat Methods* 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-024-02201-0)) went generative over gene-and-cell tokens; **(9) scFoundation** ([Hao et al., *Nat Methods* 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-024-02305-7)) made attention read-depth-aware, the first real gesture at architecture–biology co-design; **(10) UCE** ([Rosen et al., 2023](https://doi.org/10.1101/2023.11.28.568918)) pushed cross-species zero-shot embedding at 650M params; **(11) CellPLM** ([Wen et al., ICLR 2024](https://openreview.net/forum?id=BKXvPDekud)) treated whole cells as tokens to model inter-cell relations; and **(12) Nicheformer** ([Schaar et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.04.15.589472)) folded in spatial niche context. **(13) TranscriptFormer** ([Pearce et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.04.25.650731)) carried the cross-species ambition into the current generation. What every one of them really delivered was a **(14) frozen encoder**: pretrain once, then read out coordinates for any new cell with no further training — the transfer-learning payoff that made the whole field economically sane. As scaling continued, two 2025 systems set the frontier: **(15) STATE** ([Arc Institute, 2025](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.06.26.661135)), a perturbation-specialist pretrained on hundreds of millions of cells, and **(16) Tahoe-x1** ([2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.10.23.683759.full.pdf)), a 3-billion-parameter encoder trained on the Tahoe-100M drug-response atlas. By the end of act one, a cell had coordinates. The open question was whether coordinates were the same thing as understanding.

## Act II — RECKON: coordinates are not predictions (the hinge)

They were not. The test that mattered was the **(17) perturbation-prediction task**: given a drug or a gene knockout, predict the cell's new expression. In August 2025 a **(18) linear baseline** — predict the mean of the training perturbations, no FM at all — was shown by **(19) Ahlmann-Eltze & Huber** ([*Nat Methods* 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-025-02772-6)) to match or beat every published single-cell FM on exactly this task. The diagnosis was a clean split: the FMs had mastered **(20) embed-and-classify** — sorting cells by type and state — but not the **(20, cont.) counterfactual**, predicting the effect of an unseen intervention. Part of the gap was methodological — **(21) leaky splits** and metrics that rewarded predicting the population mean — and part was real. The result did not end the field; it re-pointed it. The **(22) 2026 re-appraisal** kept FMs for what they are genuinely good at (representation, integration, annotation) and stopped pretending the masked-token objective had learned regulatory logic. And it sent people inside the box: **(23) sparse autoencoders (SAE)** trained on scGPT and scFoundation embeddings ([Pedrocchi et al., ICLR 2026](https://openreview.net/forum?id=5AuN6RD072); cf. [InterPLM](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.12101) on protein models) decompose the latent into **(24) monosemantic features** — interpretable directions — and the verdict echoed the reckoning: the features encode organized biological *knowledge*, but minimal causal *mechanism*. (The full anatomy is the [reckoning essay](why-linear-baselines-win.md) and the [interpretability state-of-play](interpretability-state-of-cell-fms.md).)

## Act III — GENERATE: draw the cell, don't just label it

If a point prediction loses to a ridge regression, raise the ambition: model the whole **(25) distribution** of treated cells and sample from it. The machinery came straight from image generation. **(26) Diffusion** ([Ho et al., 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239)) learns to reverse a gradual noising process; **(27) flow matching** ([Lipman et al., 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.02747)) learns a velocity field that carries noise to data in a straighter shot; the **(28) Diffusion Transformer (DiT)** ([Peebles & Xie, 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.09748)) makes that denoiser an attention model — so act three is built *on* act one's transformer, not against it. Generating raw 20,000-dimensional counts is brutal, so **(29) latent diffusion** ([Rombach et al., 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.10752)) runs the process inside a learned latent and a **(30) count-aware decoder** (a negative-binomial head) restores the discrete, over-dispersed structure of real scRNA-seq. Steering comes from **(31) classifier-free guidance** ([Ho & Salimans, 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2207.12598)), pushing samples toward a drug or dose condition. The single-cell instances followed quickly: **(32) CFGen** ([Palma et al., ICLR 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.11734)) and **(33) scDiffusion** ([Luo et al., 2024](https://academic.oup.com/bioinformatics/article/40/9/btae518/7738782)) generate count-faithful cells; **(34) CellFlow** ([2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.04.11.648220v1)) flow-matches whole population responses; **(35) scDiffEq** ([2023](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.12.06.570508)) makes the diffusion *be* the cell's dynamics. And here the act earns its caveat: **(36) distribution-matching is not causality.** A generator can reproduce the treated population beautifully and still not know why any cell moved — the joint distribution is not the regulatory mechanism. (Two companions go deeper: the [diffusion/flow explainer](diffusion-and-flow-matching-for-virtual-cells.md) and [how transformers and diffusion compose](transformers-and-diffusion-together-in-vc.md).)

## Act IV — CAUSE → INTERVENE: model the effect, then go get it

The honest response to that caveat is to model the **effect** directly — Pearl's rung two, the **(37) do() operator**: not P(cell) but P(cell | *do*(drug)). A genuinely causal lineage exists, distinct from the generators. **(38) CPA** ([Lotfollahi et al., *Mol Syst Biol* 2023](https://doi.org/10.15252/msb.202211517)) disentangles a cell's latent into additive perturbation, dose, and covariate components; **(39) chemCPA** ([Hetzel et al., NeurIPS 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.13545)) routes the perturbation through a molecular-structure encoder so it can extrapolate to unseen drugs. **(40) GEARS** ([Roohani et al., *Nat Biotechnol* 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-023-01905-6)) injects a gene–gene knowledge graph to predict combinatorial and unseen knockouts. Others attack structure and confounding head-on: **(41) causal discovery from interventions** ([DCD-FG, Lopez et al., 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.07824)) learns a regulatory graph from Perturb-seq, **(42) CINEMA-OT** ([Dong et al., *Nat Methods* 2023](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-023-02040-5)) separates the true treatment effect from confounders with optimal transport, and **(43) Mixscape** ([Papalexi et al., *Nat Genet* 2021](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-021-00778-2)) removes non-perturbed contamination before any effect is read. The deepest causal idea is the **(44) optimal-transport coupling** as a map from control to treated population — **(44, cont.)** Waddington-OT ([Schiebinger et al., 2019](https://www.cell.com/cell/fulltext/S0092-8674(19)30039-X)), CellOT ([Bunne et al., *Nat Methods* 2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-023-01969-x)), and moscot ([Klein et al., *Nature* 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08453-2)) — which makes the perturbation a transport plan you can read as **(45) a per-cell treatment effect**. Push that to its sharpest form and you get the **(46) survival field** π(*x* | drug, dose) — predicting *who survives* rather than what survivors look like, the [bet this corpus argues for](what-disappears-conditional-viability-v3.md) — which forces a distinction the generators skip: **(47) competing risks**, separating true death from non-lethal state transition.

But every method to here is observational: it conditions on interventions someone already ran. The top of the ladder is to **run new ones on purpose.** **(48) Active experimental design** ([IterPert, Huang et al., 2023](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.12.12.571389v1)) turns the model into a policy that picks the next perturbation to maximally reduce its own uncertainty — the model finally earning causality by acting. Closing that loop is the current frontier: **(49) agentic builders** — the [Virtual Cell Challenge](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2025.06.008) framing the whole thing as a Turing test for the cell, and autonomous biomedical agents like [Biomni](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.05.30.656746) that design and triage experiments — pursued as [closed-loop, agent-orchestrated in-silico perturbation](agentic-meets-foundation.md). And the axis the first three acts mostly ignore is **(50) dynamics**: today's models are snapshots; the next is time — snapshot → trajectory → a runnable world model of the cell.

Four acts, four verbs: **represent, reckon, generate, cause.** The field's whole history is a climb up the ladder of ambition, and each rung was a confession about the one below — coordinates were not predictions, generation was not causation. The rung after generation is not a better generator. It is the will to intervene: to stop conditioning on the experiments others ran and start choosing the next one. That is the move from a model that *describes* the cell to one that, finally, *acts on* it.
