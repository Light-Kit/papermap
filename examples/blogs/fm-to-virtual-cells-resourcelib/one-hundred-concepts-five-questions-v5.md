> *The fully-cited, doubled edition of the [five-questions cut](fifty-concepts-five-questions-v4.md) — one hundred numbered concepts now, each carried once and linked to its source, but the skeleton is unchanged: the same five questions we ask a cell, each a rung higher than the last. v4 is the lean climb; this is the reference map. The deep-dives still live elsewhere: the [reckoning](why-linear-baselines-win.md), the [diffusion / flow explainer](diffusion-and-flow-matching-for-virtual-cells.md), the [causal-models survey](causal-models-fm-and-vc.md), the [interpretability state-of-play](interpretability-state-of-cell-fms.md), and the [survival-field model](what-disappears-conditional-viability-v3.md) this ladder climbs toward, argued as a [bet for a small lab](fifty-concepts-one-bet-v2.md).*

Every computational biologist is chasing the same dream, even when we don't say it out loud: to rebuild a living cell inside a computer — not a cartoon of one, but a cell you can *query*. Pin down what "query" means and the dream resolves into five questions, each harder than the last. What does a typical cell look like? Which genes move together, and in what programs? What happens when you hit it with a signal — a drug, a cytokine? Where will it be in ten days? And, at the top of the ladder: I know where it's headed — what do I do to send it somewhere else? The history of virtual-cell models is the climb up these five rungs, and the recurring mistake is taking one rung for another. One hundred concepts, in order, carried once each.

## Rung 1 — DESCRIBE: give a cell coordinates

A **(1) foundation model (FM)** is a high-capacity network pretrained once on a large unlabeled corpus and adapted to many tasks; the **(2) virtual cell (VC)** is the prize at the end — a computational cell you can ask "what if." The raw material is the **(3) single-cell atlas** ([CELLxGENE](https://cellxgene.cziscience.com/), Tabula Sapiens) — an observational catalog of which states cells happen to occupy. The recipe was borrowed from language models: **(4) self-supervised pretraining** on millions of transcriptomes, **(5) tokenization** turning a cell's expression counts into a finite vocabulary, and **(6) masked-gene modeling** — hide some of a cell's *genes* (not its labels), predict them, and learn a representation as a side effect.

The wave: **(7) scBERT** ([Yang et al., *Nat Mach Intell* 2022](https://doi.org/10.1038/s42256-022-00534-z)), BERT transposed onto genes; **(8) Geneformer** ([Theodoris et al., *Nature* 2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06139-9)), which encodes a cell as a *rank* ordering of its genes; **(9) scGPT** ([Cui et al., *Nat Methods* 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-024-02201-0)), generative over gene-and-cell tokens; **(10) scFoundation** ([Hao et al., *Nat Methods* 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-024-02305-7)), whose attention is read-depth-aware; **(11) UCE** ([Rosen et al., 2023](https://doi.org/10.1101/2023.11.28.568918)), cross-species at 650M params; **(12) CellPLM** ([Wen et al., ICLR 2024](https://openreview.net/forum?id=BKXvPDekud)), treating whole cells as tokens; **(13) Nicheformer** ([Schaar et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.04.15.589472)), folding in spatial niche; **(14) TranscriptFormer** ([Pearce et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.04.25.650731)), carrying cross-species into the current generation. Underneath sits **(15) scVI** ([Lopez et al., *Nat Methods* 2018](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-018-0229-2)), the variational autoencoder whose ELBO is still the cleanest per-cell uncertainty handle. What each really delivered was a **(16) frozen encoder**: pretrain once, read out coordinates for any new cell — enabling **(17) zero-shot transfer**, **(18) batch integration**, and **(19) cell-type annotation**. And the 2026 counter-move worth watching is the **(20) non-transformer architecture** — transcriptomics-native designs that match or beat LM-pedigree FMs, the first real evidence the transformer shape is not load-bearing. By the end of rung one, a cell had coordinates.

## Rung 2 — CORRELATE: read the programs

The next question is which genes move together. The embeddings encode **(21) gene programs** — co-expression modules — and gesture at the **(22) gene regulatory network (GRN)** beneath them. On this rung the FMs proved good at **(23) embed-and-classify**, and one reading of their power is **(24) attention as a gene–gene interaction map**: a learned, dense affinity between genes. To read the programs back out of the black box, **(25) sparse autoencoders (SAEs)** ([Pedrocchi et al., ICLR 2026](https://openreview.net/forum?id=5AuN6RD072)) decompose the latent into **(26) monosemantic features** — interpretable directions, one concept apiece — the single-cell instance of **(27) mechanistic interpretability**, with the simpler **(28) linear probe** as its baseline and [InterPLM](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.12101) as the **(29) protein-model analog**. Studying the **(30) representational geometry** of these spaces (and using FMs for **(31) imputation and denoising**) rounds out what rung two buys. But the verdict that emerged is the one rung three will deliver the hard way — **(32) knowledge without mechanism**: the features encode organized biological *knowledge* and almost no causal *mechanism*.

## The hinge — RECKON: coordinates are not predictions

The test that mattered was the **(33) perturbation-prediction task**: given a drug or knockout, predict the new state. In 2025 a **(34) linear baseline** — predict the mean of training perturbations, no FM — was shown by the reckoning of **(35) Ahlmann-Eltze, Huber & Anders** ([*Nat Methods* 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-025-02772-6)) to match or beat every published single-cell FM; the **(36) mean / additive baseline** result was independently corroborated by Kernfeld et al. (2025). Follow-ups established the **(37) latent-additive baseline** (a one-line regression on FM embeddings) as the new floor, and decomposed the failure into **(38) leaky splits**, **(39) metric (mis)calibration**, and a real gap. The **(40) 2026 re-appraisal** kept FMs for rungs one and two (once fine-tuned) and stopped pretending masked-gene pretraining had learned regulatory logic. The diagnosis: representation is not intervention, and rung three is not free.

## Rung 3 — INTERVENE: what a signal does

So the field changed its data, turning from **(41) observational to interventional data**. Genetic intervention came through **(42) Perturb-seq** ([Replogle et al., *Cell* 2022](https://www.cell.com/cell/fulltext/S0092-8674(22)00746-8)); chemical intervention through **(43) sci-Plex** ([Srivatsan et al., *Science* 2020](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aax6234)) and **(44) MIX-Seq** ([McFarland et al., *Nat Commun* 2020](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-020-17440-w)), scaling up to **(45) Tahoe-100M** ([Vevo + Arc, 2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.02.20.639398v3.full)) — ~100M cells, ~1,100 small molecules, 50 cancer lines, dose-resolved. On that substrate came perturbation-native models: **(46) STATE** ([Arc Institute, 2025](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.06.26.661135)), whose move is to model the **(47) set-level distributional shift** a perturbation induces, and the 3-billion-parameter **(48) Tahoe-x1** ([2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.10.23.683759.full.pdf)) — the **(49) substrate-as-moat** answer to the masked-token ceiling. Two lessons split the field into a **(50) two-direction era**: institutes scaling, small groups innovating — the latter winning with an **(51) adapter** (LoRA/FiLM) on a frozen backbone, the now-routine finding that **(52) fine-tuning beats pretraining**. **(53) Pertpy** standardized the analysis pipeline underneath both. A whole family answers rung three descriptively, via **(54) differential abundance** — [Milo](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-021-01033-z)'s kNN-neighborhood test, **(55) MELD** ([Burkhardt et al., *Nat Biotechnol* 2021](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-020-00803-5))'s per-cell relative likelihood, **(56) scCODA** ([Büttner et al., *Nat Commun* 2021](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-021-27150-6))'s compositional test — measuring which populations shrank. And the rung's open argument is the **(57) metrics rebuttal** ([GenBio AI, 2026](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2026.02.18.706454v1)): under better-calibrated metrics, larger models *do* beat baselines (a non-peer-reviewed, commercially interested preprint — directionally fair, not settled). The field's program is **(58) in-silico perturbation (ISP)**, with **(59) context generalization** — the [Virtual Cell Challenge](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2025.06.008)'s held-out target — and **(60) context-dependent gene function** as the prize, since the same perturbation does different things in different cells.

## Rung 4 — PREDICT: draw the cell forward (and through time)

If a point prediction loses to a ridge regression, model the whole **(61) distribution** and sample. The machinery came from computer vision: **(62) diffusion** ([Ho et al., 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239)) reverses a noising process; **(63) flow matching** ([Lipman et al., 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.02747)) learns a velocity field that carries noise to data; the **(64) Diffusion Transformer (DiT)** ([Peebles & Xie, 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.09748)) makes the denoiser an attention model — so rung four is built *on* rung one. **(65) Latent diffusion** ([Rombach et al., 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.10752)) runs it inside a learned latent; a **(66) count-aware decoder** (a negative-binomial head) restores discrete, over-dispersed counts; **(67) classifier-free guidance** ([Ho & Salimans, 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2207.12598)) steers toward a drug or dose. The single-cell instances: **(68) CFGen** ([Palma et al., ICLR 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.11734)), **(69) scDiffusion** ([Luo et al., 2024](https://academic.oup.com/bioinformatics/article/40/9/btae518/7738782)), **(70) CellFlow** ([2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.04.11.648220v1)), and **(71) scDiffEq** ([2023](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.12.06.570508)), which makes the diffusion *be* the dynamics. The other half of "forward" is real dynamics, not generative sampling: **(72) RNA velocity** ([La Manno et al., *Nature* 2018](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-018-0414-6)) reads near-future direction from spliced/unspliced ratios, refined by **(73) scVelo** ([Bergen et al., *Nat Biotechnol* 2020](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-020-0591-3)) and **(74) dynamo** ([Qiu et al., *Cell* 2022](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2021.12.045)), while **(75) trajectory inference** ([Monocle](https://doi.org/10.1038/nbt.2859), Slingshot, PAGA) orders cells along pseudotime, and **(76) RegVelo** couples velocity with a regulatory network. (Schrödinger-bridge and dose-conditioned diffusion variants are pushing the same frontier.) And the rung's caveat, the one that forces the last rung: **(77) distribution-matching is not causality** — and so a static-snapshot model can never quite resolve **(78) dynamics**, the time axis the snapshots throw away.

## Rung 5 — COUNTERFACTUAL: change its fate

The honest response is to model the *effect* — Pearl's **(79) do() operator**: not P(cell) but P(cell | *do*(drug)) — whose obstacle has a precise name, **(80) causal transportability** (a model fit under one context need not hold under another). A genuinely causal lineage exists. One thread disentangles the effect: **(81) CPA** ([Lotfollahi et al., *Mol Syst Biol* 2023](https://doi.org/10.15252/msb.202211517)) factors a latent into perturbation, dose, and covariate; **(82) chemCPA** ([Hetzel et al., NeurIPS 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.13545)) routes the perturbation through a molecular-structure encoder for unseen drugs; **(83) GEARS** ([Roohani et al., *Nat Biotechnol* 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-023-01905-6)) injects a gene-graph for unseen knockouts; **(84) PerturbNet** generalizes the generative map across perturbation space. Another attacks structure and confounding: **(85) causal discovery from interventions** ([DCD-FG, Lopez et al., 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.07824)), **(86) CINEMA-OT** ([Dong et al., *Nat Methods* 2023](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-023-02040-5)) separating effect from confounder, and **(87) Mixscape** ([Papalexi et al., *Nat Genet* 2021](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-021-00778-2)) removing non-perturbed contamination. The deepest causal idea is the **(88) optimal-transport coupling** — a map from control to treated: **(89) CellOT** ([Bunne et al., *Nat Methods* 2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-023-01969-x)) and **(90) moscot** ([Klein et al., *Nature* 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08453-2)). Crucially, its **(91) unbalanced OT** ([Chizat et al., 2018](https://arxiv.org/abs/1508.05216)) form lets mass be created and destroyed; the **(92) Wasserstein–Fisher–Rao split** ([Chizat et al., 2015](https://arxiv.org/abs/1506.06430)) separates a death term from a transport term — the machinery of [Waddington-OT](https://www.cell.com/cell/fulltext/S0092-8674(19)30039-X), **(93) NUBOT** ([Lübeck et al., 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.15621)), and **(94) TIGON** ([Sha et al., *Nat Mach Intell* 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s42256-023-00763-w)), which already learns a per-cell growth/death rate field — for a developmental time-course, not a drug. Either way, the coupling is readable as a **(95) per-cell treatment effect**.

Push that to its sharpest form and the question flips: not what survivors look like, but **who survives** — the **(96) survival field** π(*x* | drug, dose), the probability a cell at state *x* persists, which forces **(97) competing risks**, separating true death from non-lethal transition. The selection logic is old — CRISPR screens read a **(98) selection coefficient** off guide dropout ([MAGeCK, Li et al., *Genome Biol* 2014](https://doi.org/10.1186/s13059-014-0554-4)) — but per-gene and in bulk, never per-cell from transcriptomic state. The top of the ladder is to stop conditioning on others' experiments and run your own: **(99) active experimental design** ([IterPert, Huang et al., 2023](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.12.12.571389v1)), the closed loop in which a model earns causality by acting — toward **(100) a runnable world model** of the cell, snapshot → trajectory → simulator.

## So where are we, and which rung is next

We have powerful models (STATE), giant interventional atlases (Tahoe-100M), and standard pipelines (Pertpy). On the five-rung ladder, the field has climbed solidly through rung four and is just setting hands on rung five. Here is the bet this map argues for — and it is a *bet*, not consensus: the next breakthrough is not a bigger foundation model. Scaling still pays (Tahoe-x1, STATE), and the metrics rebuttal cuts the other way, so reasonable people disagree. But the most under-worked axis is not a better generator — it is a change in the *object* we model, from existence to survival. Dose-conditioning and unseen-drug generalization are now crowded; what no one has assembled is a learned, drug- and dose-conditioned, per-cell survival field that separates death from transition. The closest neighbors are partial: unbalanced-OT growth/death models (the math) and selection coefficients (the semantics), and CPA — modeling only the transcriptome of cells that survived to be sequenced — by construction cannot speak to viability or abundance at all. That gap is the opportunity. Build the model that knows who lives, and you finally have a virtual cell worth querying: not one that tells you what a treated population looks like, but one that tells you what to *do*.
