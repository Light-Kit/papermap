## The substrate — what a virtual cell foundation model eats

A **(1) foundation model (FM)** is a high-capacity neural network pretrained on a large self-supervised corpus and then adapted to many downstream tasks; the architectural lineage is the same as the language-model wave that produced GPT and BERT. A **(2) virtual cell** is the artifact the single-cell community wants on the other end of that pipeline — a computational object that lets you ask "what would this cell look like if I gave it drug X or knocked out gene Y" and get back a wet-lab-replicable answer. The substrate is **(3) single-cell atlases** — millions of observed transcriptomes from healthy donors organized into resources like **(4) Tabula Sapiens** and the Human Cell Atlas. These are observational catalogs of which states cells happen to occupy, not records of what happens when you reach in and intervene. To get intervention data you need **(5) Perturb-seq** ([Replogle et al., *Cell* 2022](https://www.cell.com/cell/fulltext/S0092-8674(22)00746-8)): single-cell RNA-seq paired with CRISPR knockouts or activations, so each cell carries a known perturbation tag. The largest perturbation atlas as of 2026 is **(6) Tahoe-100M** (Vevo + Arc, Feb 2025), a hundred million cells across 1,100 drugs and 50 cancer lines. Pretrained atlas + perturbation atlas + adapter is the modern pipeline. The substrate is what the FM eats; everything afterwards is the question of whether what comes out the other end is a cell that biology will recognize.

## Models, architectures, and the adapters bolted on

The 2023–2024 single-cell FM wave produced five anchor models. **(7) scGPT** ([Cui et al., *Nat Methods* 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-024-02201-0)) is generative, 51M params, trained on 33M cells. **(8) Geneformer** ([Theodoris et al., *Nature* 2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06139-9)) is encoder-only, masked-token, 10/104/316M params across the V2 cancer-curated checkpoints. **(9) scFoundation** is xTrimoGene encoder, 100M params, with read-depth-aware attention — the closest existing sc-FM to architecture–biology co-design. **(10) UCE** ([Rosen et al., *Nat Methods* 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-024-02201-0)) is ViT-style, 650M params, cross-species. **(11) CellPLM** is the cell-as-token variant. All five lean on **(12) masked-gene modeling** or next-gene prediction as the pretraining loss — the NLP playbook transposed to gene tokens via **(13) tokenization** schemes that map expression counts to a finite vocabulary. **(14) STATE** (Arc Institute 2025) shipped as a perturbation-specialist FM with a public adapter API, pretrained on ~170M unperturbed and ~100M perturbed cells across 70 species — the substrate-as-moat answer to the masked-token ceiling. **(15) scVI** ([Lopez et al., *Nat Methods* 2018](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-018-0229-2)) sits underneath the whole stack as the variational autoencoder whose ELBO is still the cleanest existing per-cell uncertainty handle in production.

When fine-tuning beats pretraining the bottleneck moves to the head. An **(16) adapter** — LoRA, FiLM, or scDCA-style bias modulation — is a small (<1% of params) trainable module that specializes a **(17) frozen backbone** for a downstream task without retraining the substrate. The 2026 architectural counter-move is xVERSE — a transcriptomics-native, non-LM architecture that beats LM-pedigree FMs by 12–34% on representation and imputation, the first published evidence that the transformer shape is not load-bearing.

## The reckoning, in three frames

In August 2025 **(18) Ahlmann-Eltze & Huber** ([*Nat Methods* 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-025-02772-6)) showed that a trivial **(19) linear baseline** — predict the mean of training perturbations, no FM, no fine-tune — matched or beat every published single-cell FM on perturbation prediction. The follow-up wave (Kedzierska, Wenkel, Wu, Liu, the parameter-free baseline paper, scPerturBench, scEval) established the **(20) latent-additive baseline** — a one-line linear regression on FM embeddings — as the new floor, and decomposed the failure into four overlapping causes: wrong loss objective, wrong architecture, leaky historical splits, and the empirical finding that the FM had learned cell-type identity rather than regulatory logic. The cleanest theoretical name for the failure is **(21) causal transportability** in Pearl's sense — a model trained on P(X|do(Y), Z=z₁) does not predict in P(X|do(Y), Z=z₂) when context Z changes, without structural assumptions about how Z couples to the rest. That maps directly onto **(22) Pearl's ladder of causation**: rung 1 is association (what tends to co-occur), rung 2 is intervention (what happens if I reach in), rung 3 is counterfactual. Observational atlases are rung-1; the question everyone wants — perturbation prediction — is rung-2; the gap explains everything. The field's three responses are **(23) in silico perturbation (ISP)** treated as data acquisition (better perturbation atlases), as architecture (perturbation-aware adapters), or as **(24) closed-loop ISP** orchestrated by **(25) LLM agents** that pick the next experiment — VCHarness (April 2026) is the frontier.

## Calibration, reachability, and the things FMs don't tell you

A virtual cell that does not know it does not know is not a virtual cell. The named handles are mature in ML and largely missing from the cell-FM stack. **(26) Shannon surprise** is −log p(x) under the model — a per-token novelty score the masked-gene loss already exposes but the papers do not foreground. The deeper distinction is **(27) epistemic vs aleatoric uncertainty** ([Kendall & Gal, NeurIPS 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1703.04977)): epistemic is fixable with more data, aleatoric is irreducible noise. Most of what you care about is epistemic, because that is the signal that flags extrapolation. **(28) Conformal prediction** gives distribution-free coverage guarantees on predicted response sets; **(29) calibration curves** and reliability diagrams are how you check whether a confidence is honest; **(30) OOD detection** methods — Mahalanobis distance to training features, ODIN, energy score — are the tooling. The cell side ships none of these as published heads.

Reachability is the sibling question: which states is the cell *allowed* to occupy? The dynamical-systems literature has been building handles for a decade. The **(31) Waddington landscape** is the foundational metaphor — cell states as basins separated by ridges; **(32) PRESCIENT** ([Yeo et al., *Nat Commun* 2021](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-021-23518-w)) learns the scalar potential U(x) directly from snapshot lineage data. **(33) Optimal transport** on single-cell snapshots (Waddington-OT, CellOT) reads barrier height as transport cost between unperturbed and perturbed populations; **(34) CellRank** ([Weiler et al., *Nat Methods* 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-024-02303-9)) computes absorption probabilities to terminal macrostates as an operational reachability score; **(35) RNA velocity** (scVelo, dynamo) reads turnover from spliced/unspliced ratios so that Jacobian eigenvalues at fixed points give linear stability for free. The hard data prior is **(36) lineage exclusivity** — Oct4/GATA6 and PAX6/MyoD are mutually exclusive at the master-regulator level, and no FM has been fine-tuned with a hinge loss that forbids their co-expression.

## Reading state off the embedding — stability, niches, lifespans

The stability sibling decomposed "stable" into four interpretable heads, of which one — **(37) niche dependence (λ)** — is genuinely missing from the FM stack. **(38) NicheNet** ([Browaeys et al., *Nat Methods* 2020](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-019-0667-5)) supplies per-ligand responder gene-sets; the prelim probe masks each ligand's responder set in the input and reads the embedding shift as a per-cell, per-ligand dependence vector. **(39) Ligand-receptor coupling** is the substrate-side view: each cell's niche dependencies live in the cytokines, growth factors, and matrix signals it consumes from physical neighbors. **(40) SenMayo** ([Saul et al., *Nat Commun* 2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-022-32552-1)) and apoptosis-priming signatures ([Sarosiek et al., *Cancer Cell* 2017](https://www.cell.com/cancer-cell/fulltext/S1535-6108(17)30048-9)) are the τ-side handles for lifespan; **(41) BH3 profiling** (Letai lab) is the wet-lab gold standard that any in-silico lifespan score has to match head-to-head. **(42) LARRY** ([Weinreb et al., *Science* 2020](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.aaw3381)) is the scRNA-seq lineage-tracing substrate that turns kinetic stability into a supervised regression target. Underneath everything, **(43) sparse autoencoders (SAEs)** trained on the FM residual stream ([Pedrocchi et al., bioRxiv Oct 2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.10.22.681631v2)) are the modern feature-discovery substrate — **(44) mechanistic interpretability** imported wholesale from LLM-side work at Anthropic and elsewhere. The empirical finding is hard: SAE features label ~29–59% of monosemantic units with GO terms, but only ~6–10% of transcription factors produce target-specific responses to perturbation. That **(45) regulatory logic gap** is the field's current credibility ceiling — the substrate carries co-occurring biology, not causal regulators.

## The other-modality view, and what protein-side did right

A transcriptome is a lagging indicator. Failure can live in the chromatin (lineage incompatibility), the contact map (DNA damage), the proteome (proteostasis collapse), the imaging readout (metabolic crash), the spatial neighborhood (niche decoupling), or the genome (genetic incompatibility) before the RNA reflects it. **(46) Multimodality** is therefore not a refinement of the stability question but the correct cutting point — six failure modes, six native readouts, six FMs already trained on each substrate (HiCFoundation for 3D genome, scBasset for ATAC, ESM-2 for proteins, OpenCell for organelle imaging, JUMP-CP-trained image FMs for morphology, Visium-trained spatial FMs for niche). **(47) Cell Painting** and the JUMP-CP consortium supply the morphological-imaging axis at million-image scale; **(48) Visium** plus deconvolution by cell2location and RCTD supplies the spatial axis, making the λ head measurable at the population level for the first time. The protein-side counterexamples then close the loop. **(49) AlphaFold** ([Jumper et al., *Nature* 2021](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-03819-2)) ships pLDDT and PAE as headline per-residue confidence — the protein community internalized uncertainty by construction. **(50) ESM-2** ([Lin et al., *Science* 2023](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.ade2574)) perplexity calibrates variant-effect rank order across 217 deep-mutational-scanning datasets; AlphaGenome wins 25/26 on regulatory variant effects without prompting. The protein and genomic FMs internalized uncertainty and interpretability by construction. The cell side has not. Every essay in this corpus is, in one way or another, asking what it would take to close that gap — and the [niche-dependence prelim](reading-niche-dependence-off-frozen-cell-fms.md) is the first attempt to do it in fourteen days, with two binary falsifiers and a single A100. Fifty concepts, one map; from here every other essay is a coordinate.
