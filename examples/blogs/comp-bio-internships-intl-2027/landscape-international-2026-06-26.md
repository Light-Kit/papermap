# the international landscape: where a PRC-national PhD can actually intern

this is the non-US companion to the main vault. it tracks 193 organizations and 238 internship roles across four regions — UK & Europe, China, Singapore/Japan/Korea, and Canada/Australia — read for a computational-biology PhD with a Chinese (PRC) passport who is currently in a US program and wants a summer-2027 internship outside the US. the filter here is **region**, not sector, because abroad the first question is not "what kind of company" but "can i actually work there." the same honesty caveat holds: summer-2027 postings mostly are not live yet, so these are 2026-cycle roles and recurring programs that show the shape and the eligibility.

the one thing that changes everything versus the US vault: **F-1, CPT, and OPT are irrelevant abroad.** the eligibility flag here is work-authorization in the host country for a PRC national. that single fact reorders the whole map.

## china — the highest-certainty region by far

for a PRC national this is the strongest region, because there is no work-visa barrier at all. nearly every china row is flagged work-auth `✓`, and the depth is real: **beigene's computational-science / bioinformatics interns**, wuxi, bgi and mgi (bioinformatics + stereo-seq spatial platforms), tencent ai lab's rhino-bird program, alibaba damo health, insilico medicine's ai-drug-discovery interns, and the clinical-genomics / liquid-biopsy shops (burning rock, genetron, amoy, singlera, novogene) whose NGS work maps straight onto a single-cell / HRD background. on top of industry, the university summer-research and visiting-PhD schemes are wide open — westlake, tsinghua's global program, USTC's FuSEP, zhejiang's GRIPS, SJTU's summer research, fudan's ISSF. if you want certainty of being able to take the seat, start here. the trade-off is that many china roles run on campus-recruitment timelines (生信实习 / 校招) rather than a fixed western "summer-intern" req, so you track them through company WeChat/校招 portals and lab contacts, not a careers ATS.

## uk & europe — the work-permit is the whole game

europe has the most prestigious comp-bio internships in the world and the hardest eligibility fine print for a non-EU national. the divide is sharp:

- **explicitly international-friendly programs (work-auth `✓`).** **roche's RiSE** scheme in basel accepts non-EU/EFTA PhD students and even reimburses the swiss work-permit cost — its bioinformatics & exploratory data analysis (BEDA) group is a clean single-cell / NGS fit. **EMBL and EMBL-EBI** (hinxton, heidelberg) are intergovernmental and structurally international — visiting-predoctoral, scientific-visitor, and trainee programs that routinely take non-EU students. these are the european bullseyes.
- **explicit no-sponsorship walls (work-auth `✗`).** **astrazeneca's** UK summer internship states applicants must not require sponsorship; **GSK's** industrial placement requires UK/ROI university enrollment. for a US-based PRC PhD these are dead ends unless your status changes — flagged so you do not waste the application.
- **everything else is verify.** novartis basel, sanofi/servier/ipsen in france, CSL behring (germany — explicitly takes non-EU students but you must secure the permit yourself), lonza, the EU techbio (owkin, instadeep, bioptimus). the science is excellent; the gate is whether the host will support a permit. confirm before investing.

## singapore / japan / korea — a*star is the standout

singapore's **A*STAR** (genome institute, bioinformatics institute) runs research-attachment and international-PhD programs that are explicitly open to overseas students — the most international-friendly APAC entry, and on-profile for genomics. NUS, NTU, duke-NUS add university routes. japan's **RIKEN** (IMS, AIP) and the big-university summer programs take international research students; some japanese pharma internships lean domestic and japanese-language. korea (lunit, standigm, samsung, university programs) is real but smaller and often korean-context. english-friendliness varies — A*STAR and RIKEN research are english-speaking; verify per role.

## canada / australia — visa-friendlier, but watch the student-only programs

english-language and generally more navigable on immigration. the catch is that many of the flagship academic schemes are aimed at domestic students: **garvan's summer scholarship** and **WEHI's** student projects are excellent science but largely target australian-enrolled students, so they sit at `verify` for an external PhD. the more actionable routes are negotiated visiting-PhD arrangements and the techbio names — abcellera (vancouver), repare (montreal), and the toronto AI-bio cluster (vector, deep genomics, proteinqure, phenomic). canada's intern/co-op work permits are comparatively accessible.

## what i think you should do

1. **anchor on china for certainty.** it is the only region where work-authorization is a non-issue for you, and the comp-bio / genomics depth is real. build the spine here — beigene, bgi/mgi, the techbio and dx names, plus two or three university summer programs.
2. **in europe, go straight for the international-by-design programs** — EMBL / EMBL-EBI and roche RiSE — and skip the explicit no-sponsorship walls (AZ UK, GSK). treat the rest as verify-first.
3. **add A*STAR (singapore) and RIKEN (japan)** as strong, international-friendly APAC bets.
4. **treat canada/australia as visa-friendly but read the student-eligibility line** — prefer negotiated visiting-PhD roles and techbio over domestic-only scholarships.
5. **everywhere outside china, the permit/host-support question is the real screen** — settle it early, the same way the US playbook says to settle CPT/OPT before you accept anything.

filter resources by region and by the topics you actually do, and start with the starred bullseye roles. the US vault is separate; this one is for when you want to look beyond it.
