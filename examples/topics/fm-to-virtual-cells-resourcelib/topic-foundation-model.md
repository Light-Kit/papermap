Biology foundation models split into five families — single-cell, pathology, genomic, protein, multimodal — and the corpus carries 157 items touching that paradigm. The Aug 2025 reckoning (Ahlmann-Eltze, Wenkel, Wu, Liu sc-Eval, parameter-free) showed current sc-FMs don't beat linear baselines on perturbation prediction. The Feb 2026 contrarian voice (FMs Improve Perturbation) argued the picture changes with enough data. Theis 2026 and Rao 2026 are the position papers calling for a forward path: stop scaling monoliths, unify the five families. xVERSE shows the architectural choice is load-bearing (+17.9 % over LM-derived sc-FMs); TxPert is "the reckoning answering itself" (Wenkel co-authored both critique and response). The agentic intersection (Li agentic, vcHarness, CellVoyager) is the most commercially fundable lane in the 2026 menu.
