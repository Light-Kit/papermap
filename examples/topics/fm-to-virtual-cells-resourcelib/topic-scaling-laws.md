Scaling laws give the quantitative spine to "can a small lab compete on training?". Chinchilla (Hoffmann et al. 2022) is the compute-optimal scaling law behind every "under/over-trained?" judgement on a biology FM. Epoch AI's FLOPs ≈ 6 × params × tokens heuristic is what the talk's cost estimates use. Cottier 2024 (frontier-training costs) gives the economic backdrop for the compute-disclosure argument: training costs are growing 2.4×/year. NVIDIA BioNeMo sets the compute-disclosure norm and is co-marketed with Geneformer V2 — the industry channel that pushes biology labs toward transparent compute reporting. The May 2026 lane analysis: training a frontier biology FM from scratch is now off the menu for most academic labs; the open lanes are architecture (Track 4), evaluation (Tracks 3/6/8/9), data curation (Lane 5), and the agentic intersection (Lane 9).
