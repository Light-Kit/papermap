Competitions are the community's clock for the field. The Virtual Cell Challenge (Arc Institute, 2025) was the first formal VC evaluation — community benchmark with $50k prize, 5000+ teams; the result that crystallised the reckoning was that most of 300+ submissions scored worse than the mean baseline on MAE, and the winner used a hybrid of protein embeddings + classical features. CZI VCI 2025 is the annual benchmark cycle whose 2025 launch crystallised the field around shared eval. Myllia Echoes Challenge (2026) is the biotech-run successor focused on perturbation prediction — commercial entry into a previously-academic benchmark calendar. The pattern these three set: every winter the field re-runs the same task on bigger data and slightly cleaner splits, and the leaderboard tells you whether the architectural response wave actually moved the needle.
